> In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate.

This manual is the single source of truth for the visual, verbal, and experiential design of Sunnah Remedies. It is written so that a world-class design team, present or future, may build and maintain the institution without guessing. Where this manual is precise, its precision is binding. Where it states a principle, that principle governs cases the manual did not foresee. Nothing essential is left to interpretation; where interpretation is unavoidable, the interpreter is directed to the Institutional Standard (§4) and the founding statement, and must be able to defend the choice before both.

---

## How to use this manual

- **Read Parts One to Three before designing anything.** They are the institution itself; the rest is its execution. A designer who knows the tokens but not the Standard will build something that looks right and is wrong.
- **Treat every numbered clause (§) as an instruction.** "The institution does…" and "must" are binding. "Should" allows judgement, exercised against the Standard. "May" is permitted latitude.
- **Values are exact.** A hex value, a size, a ratio, or a duration in a specification table is the value to use, not an approximation.
- **When two clauses appear to conflict,** the more specific governs the more general; if still unresolved, the earlier Part governs the later, because the earlier Parts are more foundational.
- **This manual forbids as firmly as it prescribes.** The lists of refused trends, refused words, and refused patterns are as binding as any positive rule.

## The status and governance of this manual

§0.1 — This manual is a living instrument under a fixed constitution. Its **foundations do not change**: that healing is from Allah, that the tradition is a trust, that beauty is an obligation, that knowledge precedes commerce, and that the institution is built to be inherited. Everything else may be amended only through the process in §41.

§0.2 — No individual designer, engineer, or manager may override this manual by preference, deadline, or authority. Amendments are made to the manual, in the open, and dated — never applied silently to the product.

§0.3 — Every design decision in the product must be traceable to a clause in this manual. Where no clause applies, the decision is escalated to amend the manual, not resolved privately.

# PART ONE — THE INSTITUTION
*Pages 9–22*

The institution before its interface. A designer who internalises this Part will make correct decisions the manual never anticipated.

## Chapter 1 — Mission, Vision, Purpose

§1.1 **Mission.** To revive, preserve, and advance the authentic tradition of Prophetic Medicine — restoring it to a discipline of knowledge, transmitted with care, practised with excellence, offered in trust. *To revive* is to return the neglected to living practice; *to preserve* is to protect it, sourced and unaltered, from distortion; *to advance* is to carry it forward with honest inquiry, without departing from its roots.

§1.2 **Vision.** Within a generation, to stand as the world's foremost institution for Prophetic Medicine — measured not in revenue but in trust: institutionally permanent (built on the *waqf* model to be inherited whole), educationally authoritative, clinically responsible, globally present without dilution, and spiritually oriented toward returning people to their Lord through the care of the body.

§1.3 **Purpose.** To reconnect people with authentic Prophetic wellbeing, under three non-negotiable orderings: **knowledge before products, service before profit, trust before growth.** Medicine stands under *ḥifẓ al-nafs* — the preservation of life — and is therefore an act of religion, not a trade like other trades.

§1.4 **Design consequence.** Because the institution is measured in trust and built to be inherited, the design must (a) never optimise for conversion at the expense of trust, (b) never depend on anything — a trend, a platform, a technique — that will not last a century, and (c) be fully documented, so it can be handed on. These three consequences recur throughout this manual as binding constraints.

## Chapter 2 — Values & Core Principles

§2.1 **The nine values** govern the institution's character and, through it, its design. Each is a discipline the design owes.

| Value | Arabic | The design owes |
|---|---|---|
| Unity | Tawḥīd | One coherent system; nothing placed above the whole; the cure never above the Curer |
| Truthfulness | Ṣidq | No visual or verbal claim beyond the evidence; no exaggeration in image or word |
| Stewardship | Amāna | The reader's attention, data, and trust guarded, never exploited |
| Excellence & beauty | Iḥsān | Everything finished to a standard worthy of the name; ugliness is a failure of the trust |
| Grounded knowledge | ʿIlm | Sourced, graded, competent; provenance visible |
| Mercy | Raḥma | Calm, gentle, forgiving experiences for people who may be unwell |
| Wisdom | Ḥikma | Judgement of what to include and, more, what to leave out |
| Service | Khidma | The interface serves the person's intent and gets out of the way |
| Scrupulousness | Waraʿ | Where a claim or a device is doubtful, the institution keeps well back |

§2.2 **The nine core principles** (the standing maxims; applied to every case):

1. Healing is from Allah; the remedy is a means.
2. The means is pursued; the result is surrendered.
3. Nothing is attributed that cannot be traced.
4. What is proven is separated from what is reported, and both from what is merely tried.
5. We do not trade in fear.
6. Beauty is an obligation, not an ornament.
7. The body has a right, and the soul has a right.
8. The person precedes the protocol; the human precedes the sale.
9. Permanence is built by refusal.

§2.3 — Principles 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, and 9 have direct and constant design force and are cited throughout this manual. A designer should be able to name which principle any contested decision serves.

## Chapter 3 — The Philosophies, reduced to operative rules

The institution's philosophies (design, writing, photography, interaction, clinical, commerce, research) are stated in full in the Constitution. Here they are reduced to the operative rules a design team applies daily.

§3.1 **Design** (from *iḥsān*): beauty is owed; restraint over expression; the mark is a seal, not a logo; materials and forms that age; space is courtesy; the system outranks the page.

§3.2 **Writing** (from *ṣidq*): attestation not persuasion; cite or be silent; plainness as a virtue; the reader as an intelligent adult; no hyperbole.

§3.3 **Photography** (the created world as *āyāt*): stillness; the dignity of matter; modesty; natural light; images that survive decades.

§3.4 **Interaction** (from *adab* and *diyāfa*): the unhurried encounter; the interface as *khādim* (servant); never manipulate; a person's attention is a trust.

§3.5 **Clinical**: healing from Allah; the whole person; first, no harm; the patient received as a guest; never promise a cure.

§3.6 **Commerce**: the remedy priced as a means, never a miracle; provenance disclosed; the desperate never exploited; trade serves the trust.

§3.7 **Research**: transmission, reason, experience, in order; the isnād discipline applied to evidence; failure reported; pseudoscience refused; certainty never manufactured.

## Chapter 4 — The Institutional Standard *(the design decision framework)*

§4.1 — **Every design decision — of layout, colour, copy, component, image, motion, or policy — must pass this gate before it enters the institution's name.** Each question is answered honestly; a single clear "no" disqualifies.

1. **Is this authentic?** Traceable to a sound source, and represented as exactly what it is?
2. **Is this beneficial?** Serving a real need, not a manufactured want?
3. **Is this truthful?** Within the evidence, free of exaggeration, honest about limits?
4. **Is this clinically responsible?** Safe, competent, free of any promise of cure or risk of harm?
5. **Would this honour the Sunnah?** Worthy of the name, in substance and manner?
6. **Would this make future generations proud?** Or ashamed?
7. **Would we still publish this in fifty years?** Built on the durable, or the expiring?

§4.2 — A decision that fails the gate is **not softened until it passes.** It is set aside. This is the single most important operational rule in the manual.

§4.3 — The Standard is the tie-breaker for every ambiguity this manual leaves open. It is reproduced on the first page of every team's working space.

# PART TWO — ARCHITECTURE & INFORMATION MODEL
*Pages 23–36*

## Chapter 5 — One institution, three departments

§5.1 — The institution is **one house with three departments**, not three businesses under one name. The departments are, in fixed order:

| # | Department | Function | Organ | Register |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | **The Apothecary** | Provision | Hand | Descriptive, sober, provenance-forward |
| II | **The Academy** | Transmission | Mind | Scholarly, patient, cited |
| III | **Sacred Journeys** | Embodiment | Soul | Reverent, grounded, spacious |

§5.2 — Clinical care (**Consultations**) is **not a fourth department.** It is a cross-cutting clinical relationship, reached from the masthead, expressed chiefly through the Apothecary and Consultations, and held to the clinical philosophy throughout.

§5.3 — Each department's role in the mission is fixed and must be legible in its design: the Academy grounds (authority), the Apothecary provides (the material means), Sacred Journeys embodies (the lived practice). No department may present itself as independent of the other two.

## Chapter 6 — The Connective Model

§6.1 — The institution is navigated as a building: **Threshold → Corridors → Pathways.**

§6.2 **The Threshold** (the home) is the entrance hall — not a department. It receives, declares, and directs. It holds no content of its own beyond declaration and direction. Returning to the seal, from anywhere, returns the visitor here.

§6.3 **The Corridors** are the constant chrome (§7.2) — masthead, footer, type, palette, grid, voice, manners — identical everywhere. Because the corridors never change, the visitor never experiences "a new site." This is the mechanical guarantee of one institution.

§6.4 **The Pathways** are the lateral cross-references between departments, following the mission-logic **knowledge → means → embodiment.** A lesson surfaces its remedy and its journey; a remedy links to its teaching; a journey draws on both. Pathways are styled as a scholar's "see also," never as commercial cross-sell (§29.5).

## Chapter 7 — The Constant / Variable Law

§7.1 — This law is the whole answer to "how do three departments and three breakpoints remain one institution."

§7.2 **Constant across all departments and breakpoints (never varies):** the seal; the palette; the typefaces; the spacing scale and grid discipline; the navigation shell (masthead + footer); the voice's character; the interaction ethic; the absence of all pressure devices.

§7.3 **Permitted to vary — the only permitted differences:** the subject-world and treatment of **photography**; the **density and pacing** of content; the **tonal temperature** within the one palette (achieved through imagery and material accents, never a new brand colour); and the **register** of the voice within the one character.

§7.4 — **No department may receive its own logo, typeface, brand colour, or navigation pattern.** Distinctness is earned only through imagery, rhythm, and register. Any proposal that breaks §7.4 is rejected without further review.

## Chapter 8 — Information Architecture

§8.1 **The page inventory.** The institution comprises these page types. Each is fully specified in Part Seven and the wireframe record; here they are enumerated with their purpose and parent.

| Ref | Page | Parent | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| H | The Threshold (Home) | root | Receive & direct |
| C0 | The Founding Charter | Institution | The constitution, readable |
| A1 | The Academy — Index | root | Reading-room entrance |
| A2 | Academy — Subject Listing | A1 | Texts within a subject |
| A3 | Academy — The Text | A2 | Critical-edition reading view |
| A4 | Academy — Course / Enrolment | A1 | Structured study |
| P1 | The Apothecary — Index | root | The ordered cabinet |
| P2 | Apothecary — Remedy Monograph | P1 | One sourced remedy |
| P3 | Apothecary — The Counter | P2 | Review & acquisition |

| R1 | The Register | footer | Search across the corpus |
| K1 | Correspondence | footer | Human channels |
| U | Utility (404 / error / empty) | — | System states |

§8.2 **Naming & URL standard.** Paths are lowercase, hyphenated, and named in the institution's own terms, never the shop's: `/the-apothecary`, `/the-academy`, `/sacred-journeys`, `/consultations`, `/the-register`, `/charter`. A remedy lives at `/the-apothecary/[remedy-name]`; a text at `/the-academy/[subject]/[text-name]`. No tracking parameters are ever placed in a shared or printed URL. Personal or sensitive data is never placed in a URL (§40.9).

§8.3 **The content model.** Every content type carries the fields the institution's discipline requires. These are mandatory data, not optional adornment.

- **Remedy:** name (English), transliteration, botanical name, one-line nature, *basis* (statement + grade + source), known uses (bounded), honest limits, preparation, origin, price, images, pathways (to text, to journey).
- **Text / Lesson:** title, subject, level, *grade*, source(s)/isnād, body, marginal apparatus, further reading, pathways.
- **Course:** title, what-it-is, for-whom, what-it-asks, syllabus (sourced modules), teacher (name + licence/*ijāza* + chain), fee, duration.
- **Journey:** name, meaning, integrity (leader + grounding + practices), reality (itinerary, demands, cost), enquiry channel, images, pathways.
- **Source (Register):** work, author, era, standing, how referenced.

§8.4 — **A content record that lacks its mandatory provenance fields is incomplete and must not be published.** The CMS enforces this: a remedy without a graded basis, or a text without a source, cannot go live. This is principle §2.2.3 enforced in the data layer.

§8.5 **Wayfinding constants.** Every page carries: the masthead (§31), a running head naming the section, a folio mark, the footer (§31), and — where a claim appears — its source-mark (§32). These are not optional per page; they are the institution's letterhead.

# PART THREE — VOICE & EDITORIAL STANDARD
*Pages 37–52*

The institution has one voice, in the register of attestation, not persuasion. Part Three governs every word in the product, from a headline to an error string.

## Chapter 9 — The Eight Laws of the Voice

§9.1 — Binding on all copy:

1. **Testify; do not sell.** State what a thing is; never urge toward it.
2. **Say only what is true, and no more.** Hyperbole is a species of lying.
3. **Knowledge before the offer.** Lead with understanding and provenance; the offer comes after.
4. **Never frighten.** No fear, no urgency, no scarcity.
5. **Cite, or be silent.** Every claim carries its chain; the ungraded is not asserted.
6. **Promise a means, never a cure.** Healing is from Allah.
7. **Address an intelligent adult.** No flattery, no simplification into falsehood.
8. **End on the truth, not a hook.** No closing pressure, no manufactured next step.

§9.2 **Person & tense.** The institution is "we," or in its gravest register "the institution." The reader is "you," used sparingly and with dignity. Tense is present, as timeless truths are written.

## Chapter 10 — Lexicon & Mechanics

§10.1 **Words the institution keeps:** revive, preserve, advance, tradition, trust, *amāna*, means, sourced, traced, established, reported, offered, received, entrusted, scholarship, provenance, considered, in time, honestly, within the evidence, the Sunnah, a right of the community.

§10.2 **Words the institution refuses** (binding prohibition):

| Marketing hype | Urgency / scarcity | False health claim |
|---|---|---|
| discover, unlock, transform, boost, supercharge, game-changing, must-have, ultimate, secret, hack, powerful, amazing, revolutionary, elevate | hurry, limited time, don't miss, act now, last chance, while stocks last, selling fast, exclusive offer, claim your spot | cure, heals, treats, eliminates, guaranteed results, clinically proven, miracle, detox, superfood, fixes, reverses |

§10.3 — **The health-claim prohibition is absolute.** Nothing the institution sells is ever said to *cure, treat, heal,* or *eliminate* a condition. Permitted bounded forms only: *offered as a means · traditionally taken for · the tradition associates it with · within the evidence, used for.*

§10.4 **Mechanics** (binding):
- Sentence case always; proper names of departments keep their capital (The Apothecary, The Academy, Sacred Journeys).
- No exclamation marks, ever.
- The em-dash is the mark of considered qualification.
- Numerals: Roman for the institutional and the dated (Est. MMXXV, folios, I/II/III); Arabic for data, prices, quantities.
- The honorific ﷺ follows mention of the Prophet.
- Citations written *grade · collection · book*.
- The three grades (Established, Reported, Tried) capitalised as terms.

## Chapter 11 — Copy Banks

§11.1 — Usable, binding-in-spirit reference language for each surface. Writers lift and adapt; they do not dilute.

**Headlines** name or declare; never promise or exclaim. E.g. *An institution for the revival of Prophetic Medicine · This is not a clinic, a shop, or an academy · Knowledge before commerce · Nothing is attributed that cannot be traced · Built to be inherited · Remedies, traced to their source · The tradition, handed on whole · The tradition, lived.*

**Sub-headlines** qualify with sober precision, often in reflective italic. E.g. *Held to the standard of scholarship — never the standard of the marketplace · We offer a means, and never sell a miracle · Sourced, graded, and offered in trust · Every claim carries its chain.*

**Action language** (the "CTA" replaced): the institution opens doors and extends invitations. Conversion standard:

| Marketing | The institution |
|---|---|
| Shop now | Enter the Apothecary |
| Buy now / Add to cart | Add to the counter |
| Sign up / Enroll | Enrol |
| Book now | Request a consultation |
| Learn more | Read the monograph · Read on · Continue |
| Get started | Begin in the Academy |
| Contact us | Write to us |
| Download | Take the text |
| Claim your spot | Register your interest |
| Don't miss out | *(forbidden — no equivalent)* |

**Educational** — the patient teacher and precise scholar: give the reason and the source; grade the evidence; define Arabic terms; name uncertainty as a strength ("The tradition does not settle this. We tell you so rather than pretend.").

**Product** — descriptive, in monograph order (Name → Nature → Basis → Known uses → Honest limits → Preparation & source → Counter). Limits are stated ("It is a means, not a cure… a remedy is not a substitute for a physician").

**Clinical** — the patient received as a guest; limits stated up front; the practitioner's grounding shown; *Request a consultation*, never *Book now*; confirmation: "Your request is received. We will write to you within [n] days."

**Academy** — free essentials framed as a right of the community; course order *what it is · for whom · what it asks*; the teacher's *ijāza* and chain shown; *Enrol* with a no-pressure line ("There is no need to decide today. The tradition keeps.").

**Sacred Journeys** — meaning before logistics; "a journey, not a holiday"; honest reality including cost and difficulty; *Enquire · Register your interest*, never *Book*.

**Errors & system** — correct without scolding, explain and guide, in the institution's voice (see §38 for the full string set).

**Navigation** — named by what things are: The Apothecary · The Academy · Sacred Journeys · Consultations · The Register · The Founding Charter · Our Covenant · Write to us. The basket is *the Counter*; items are *your selection*; account is *your record*; there is no wishlist.

## Chapter 12 — Editorial & Citation Standard

§12.1 **The grades.** Every claim about the tradition is assigned one grade, shown with it:
- **Established** — grounded in soundly transmitted revelation and reliable tradition; stated with confidence.
- **Reported** — narrated, of lesser certainty; named as such, never dressed as more.
- **Tried** — known through careful experience rather than transmission; offered within that limit.

§12.2 **Citation form.** *Grade · Collection/Work · Book/Location.* Example: "Established · Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī · Kitāb al-Ṭibb." Where a claim rests on a chain, the chain (isnād) may be shown in the apparatus (§32). A claim with no traceable source is not published (§8.4).

§12.3 **Quotation.** Quotations of hadith and scripture are given as careful translations, attributed, and — for the Qur'an — cited by sūra name and āya number. The institution does not attach a hadith to a claim it has not verified, and does not put words into the mouths of scholars.

§12.4 **Bilingual setting.** Arabic terms are transliterated with diacritics per Appendix B, set in italic on first use, and defined. Arabic script (Qur'an, hadith, names) is set in the Arabic face (§20), marked as Arabic and right-to-left, never letterspaced or italicised. English and Arabic never compete for the same emphasis in one line.

§12.5 **Tone of correction.** Where the institution corrects a common error (a misattributed quotation, a folk claim), it does so without contempt: it states the sound position, acknowledges what is true in the error, and cites. It never mocks the mistaken.

§13.3 — **There is no alarm colour.** Oxblood, desaturated, is reserved strictly for genuine form errors (§30.6). It is never used for emphasis, decoration, or urgency. The institution corrects; it does not frighten.

## Chapter 14 — Ground Pairings

§14.1 — Only these text-on-ground pairings are permitted:

| Ground | Body text | Secondary | Accent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bone / Leaf | Ink | Taupe | Gilt (titles, rules, marks only) |
| Myrtle / Deep | Bone | Dim (#CFC9B8) | Gilt Soft |

§14.2 — Ash (#9A9081) is **decorative meta only** (folios, faint labels) and never carries essential text (§15.4).

§14.3 — The **myrtle grounds are rationed to moments of gravity** (the governing principle, the covenant, the colophon, the mobile nav interior). Myrtle is never used merely as an "alternate section colour"; its scarcity is its authority.

## Chapter 15 — Contrast Standard *(binding accessibility)*

§15.1 — The institution meets **WCAG 2.1 AA as a floor**, and AAA wherever its palette allows, which is most places.

§15.2 — Verified pairings and their permitted use:

| Pairing | Ratio | Standing | Permitted use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ink on Bone | ≈ 10.5:1 | AAA | All text |
| Bone on Myrtle | ≈ 10:1 | AAA | All text |
| Taupe on Bone | ≈ 4.6:1 | AA | Body & secondary text |
| Gilt Soft on Myrtle | ≈ 4.8:1 | AA | Links & accent text on dark |
| Gilt on Bone | ≈ 3.4:1 | AA large / UI | Titles ≥24px, rules, marks, icons — never small body |
| Ash on Bone | ≈ 2.8:1 | Fail (text) | Decorative meta only; never essential text |

§15.3 — **Any new colour pairing must be measured and recorded here before use.** A pairing that fails AA for its intended text size is not used.

§15.4 — **The gilt restriction is a hard rule.** Because Gilt on Bone is ≈3.4:1, gilt may be used for text only at large display sizes (≥24px) or as a non-text UI accent (rules, marks, icons, focus rings). Small body copy, captions, and form hints are never gilt on paper. This clause exists to prevent the single most likely accessibility regression in this palette.

## Chapter 16 — Application

§16.1 **By department (tonal temperature only, within one palette):** the Apothecary reads warmer (achieved through still-life imagery and material accents — amber, honey, olivewood); the Academy reads cooler and inkier (manuscript imagery, more text on Bone); Sacred Journeys reads atmospheric (landscape imagery, light and stone). No department changes the palette itself (§7.4).

§16.2 **By component:** rules and hairlines use Rule / Rule Dark; the seal and source-marks use Gilt / Gilt Soft; the solid action uses Myrtle ground filling to Gilt on hover; focus rings use Gilt; error text uses Oxblood.

§16.3 **Forbidden colour usages:** gradients of any kind; drop-shadow tints; coloured "success green" or "alert red" system colours (states are carried by the institution's own palette and by words, not by traffic-light colour — see §38.4); any saturated or neon accent; any second metal; dark-mode inversion as a gimmick (the institution's grounds are fixed by intent, not toggled).

# PART FIVE — TYPOGRAPHY
*Pages 65–80*

Typography carries the institution's personality: classical, scholarly, permanent. Two serifs, one quiet grotesque, one Arabic face.

## Chapter 17 — Families & Roles

§17.1 — The four faces, their roles, and their bounds:

| Role | Family | Weights | Used for | Never used for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display | Cormorant Garamond | 300–500 (+ italic) | Declarations, section titles | Body copy, small labels |
| Body | EB Garamond | 400–500 (+ italic) | All reading; the critical-edition register | Loud display; UI labels |
| Utility | Inter | 400–500 | Eyebrows, folios, nav, data, marks | Anything above ~13px |
| Arabic | Amiri | regular (+ italic) | Qur'an, hadith, names, terms | Latin text |

§17.2 **Licensing & fallbacks.** All four are open-licence (SIL OFL). The institution self-hosts them; it does not depend on a third-party font network that could fail or change terms in twenty years. Fallback stack: Cormorant/EB Garamond → Georgia → serif; Inter → system-ui → sans-serif; Amiri → "Noto Naskh Arabic" → serif. Fallbacks are specified so that a font failure degrades with dignity, never to a default sans for reading text.

§17.3 — **No further typeface may be introduced.** Additional voices are found within these four (weight, size, italic, case), never by adding a fifth face.

## Chapter 18 — The Type Scale

§18.1 — The scale is fixed. Sizes are given at desktop; the responsive rule is in §25. Line-height and tracking are integral to each token and not to be altered.

§18.2 — Weight is used with restraint: Cormorant 300 for the largest, most ceremonial lines; 400–500 as size decreases. Body never exceeds 500. Bold is not used in reading text; emphasis is italic (§19.4).

## Chapter 19 — Setting Rules

§19.1 **Measure.** Reading measure is held at **~34rem (≈66 characters)** at every breakpoint. Text is never set wider than 75 characters or narrower than 45.

§19.2 **Alignment.** Reading text is set flush-left, ragged-right. Justified text is not used (it produces rivers and an over-formal, brittle texture). Centred text is reserved for the threshold, the ayah blocks, and section closers.

§19.3 **Hyphenation & widows.** Hyphenation is off for display and headings, on for justified-risk reading columns only where it improves rag. No heading ends a line on a single word or breaks a proper name or an Arabic transliteration across lines. Widowed single words at paragraph end are avoided in display sizes.

§19.4 **Italics.** Italic is *meaning*, not emphasis: the institution's reflective voice, transliteration, work titles, and the Latin names of species. Italic is never used to shout.

§19.5 **Numerals & marks.** Old-style figures in reading text; lining figures in tables and data. The em-dash (—) for qualification, unspaced; the en-dash for ranges. Primes and true quotation marks always; no straight quotes.

## Chapter 20 — The Arabic Standard

§20.1 — Arabic is set in **Amiri**, a classical Naskh, at a size optically matched to the adjacent Latin (Arabic typically set ~10–15% larger for equal presence). It is marked `Arabic, right-to-left` at the structural level.

§20.2 — Arabic is **never** letterspaced, italicised, or set in all-caps (Arabic has no case). It is not artificially bolded; weight comes from the face.

§20.3 — Qur'anic text carries full diacritics (tashkīl); it is set with generous line-height (≥1.9) and never cropped or overlapped by other elements. The ayah block (§21.5) is its canonical container.

§20.4 — When Arabic and Latin appear in one line (a term and its gloss), the line's base direction is set by the surrounding language; the Arabic run is isolated so it renders correctly and never reverses adjacent punctuation.

## Chapter 21 — Typographic Components

§21.1 **Running head.** A top rule bearing the section name (Inter Eyebrow, left) and the folio (Folio token, right). Present on every content leaf. It is wayfinding, not decoration.

§21.2 **Folio.** The leaf's mark of place (Roman for institutional pages, lowercase roman for reading leaves). Always present; never a page number styled as a "step N of M."

§21.3 **Eyebrow.** A single Inter-Eyebrow label naming a region. One per region; it labels and does nothing else.

§21.4 **Drop-folio (chapter opener).** The opening paragraph of a long text may begin with a raised, enlarged initial or a folio mark, signalling the start of a "chapter" of the bound charter. Used once per text, at the true opening only.

§21.5 **Ayah / scripture block.** Centred; Arabic (Amiri) with tashkīl; the translation in reflective italic beneath; the citation in Micro below (sūra name · number). Always placed at the *foot* of a section, as its seal — never as a headline.

§21.6 **Citation & source-mark.** In-line claims carry a source-mark (Micro siglum, gilt) expanding to the source; block claims use the specimen apparatus (§32). Citation form per §12.2.

§21.7 **Pull-quote / grave line.** Display L on a myrtle ground, rationed to the institution's few load-bearing convictions. Never used for ordinary emphasis.

# PART SIX — SPACE, GRID & LAYOUT
*Pages 81–92*

Space is how the institution communicates permanence. Generosity is composure, not emptiness.

## Chapter 22 — The Spacing Scale

§22.1 — One scale governs all spacing. Values are not interpolated between steps except where the responsive clamp (§25) requires.

| Token | Value | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| s1 | 0.5rem | Tight inline gaps |
| s2 | 0.75rem | Label-to-value |
| s3 | 1rem | Paragraph rhythm, small gaps |
| s4 | 1.5rem | Component internal padding |
| s5 | 2.5rem | Block separation |
| s6 | 4rem | Sub-section separation |
| s7 | 6rem | Section (leaf) minimum |
| s8 | 9rem | Section (leaf) maximum / grand pauses |

§22.2 **Section gap.** Leaf padding is `clamp(s7 → s8)` at desktop, reducing to ~4rem at mobile. Section gaps are large by intent (§3.1): the institution would rather show one thing with dignity than many in haste.

§22.3 **Vertical rhythm.** Reading text sits on a consistent rhythm derived from Body L line-height. Headings take space above proportional to their size (a Display L takes ≥s6 above). The rhythm is constant across breakpoints; only scaffolding reflows (§25).

## Chapter 23 — The Grid

§23.1 — Three grids, one measure:

| Band | Range | Columns | Outer margin | Gutter | Reading measure |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Desktop | ≥1024px | 12 | clamp 3–7rem | 24px | ~34rem |
| Tablet | 640–1023px | 8 | ~2.5rem | 20px | ~32rem |
| Mobile | <640px | 4 | ~1.5rem | 16px | full-width, ~40ch |

§23.2 **Content widths.** Wide institutional bands (department grids, footer) use up to ~78rem. Reading columns hold the measure (§19.1) regardless of band width. A wide screen adds margin and scaffolding, never wider text.

§23.3 **The measure is protected absolutely.** No layout stretches reading text to fill a wide viewport. This is the mechanical root of "reading feels identical everywhere" (§25.1).

## Chapter 24 — The Leaf

§24.1 — The **leaf** is the institution's section container: full-bleed ground, generous vertical padding (§22.2), an optional running head, content held to the appropriate width, hairline rules between articles.

§24.2 **Hairlines.** Depth and division are carried by 1px hairlines (Rule / Rule Dark) and by ground changes — **never by drop shadows or elevation** (§16.3, §28.4). A hairline is 1px; it does not thicken to "emphasise."

§24.3 **Corners.** Radius is **0** everywhere. The institution uses the right angle of the printed page. No component is rounded.

§24.4 **The grave leaf.** Myrtle-ground leaves (§14.3) are rationed to gravity. Their internal rules are Rule Dark; their accent is Gilt Soft; their text is Bone / Dim.

## Chapter 25 — The Responsive Law

§25.1 — Five rules keep three widths one institution. They are binding.

1. **The reading measure is sacred.** It holds; scaffolding reflows around it.
2. **Order is the argument; order never changes.** Sections keep their sequence across all widths (definition before navigation; knowledge before commerce; source before price; meaning before booking). Reflow may turn columns into rows, never first into second.
3. **The apparatus relocates, never disappears.** Provenance (grades, isnād notes, source-marks) moves margin → footnote → inline-expand as width shrinks. On no device is sourcing dropped to save space.
4. **Commerce stays beneath knowledge at every width.** Enrol panels, counters, and booking actions relocate (side → below → sticky-slim) but are never lifted above the knowledge and provenance that precede them.
5. **Chrome and manners are invariant.** Masthead, footer, seal, type, rhythm, the quiet-link grammar, and the absence of pressure devices are identical on every page and screen.

§25.2 — **Where any breakpoint decision would break one of these five, the decision is wrong, not the rule.**

# PART SEVEN — COMPONENTS
*Pages 93–116*

Every reusable part, specified with its anatomy, states, measurements, and rules. Nothing here is left to interpretation.

## Chapter 26 — The Seal

§26.1 **Meaning.** The seal is the institution's mark of *authentication* — a *khatam*, not a logo. It certifies; it does not advertise. It is also the "return to the threshold" control.

§26.2 **Construction.** An eight-point star (two overlaid squares — the *rub-el-hizb* geometry) centred within a fine double ring. Proportions, on a 200-unit artboard: outer ring r=96, inner ring r=80 (both hairline; outer 1 unit, inner 0.6); the two squares each 48×48 units centred, one axis-aligned and one rotated 45°; an optional inner circle r=9. The full seal adds arced text on the upper ring (institution name) and lower ring (Est. MMXXV).

§26.3 **Sizes.** Full (≥88px — threshold, with arced text); mid (40–56px — section closers, footer); small (28–32px — masthead chrome, marks only, no arced text). Below 28px the seal is not used.

§26.4 **Colour.** The seal inherits text colour (renders in Gilt on paper, Gilt Soft on myrtle). It is never filled, never given a background plate, never colourised outside the palette.

§26.5 **Clear space.** Minimum clear space equal to the height of one inner-square side on all sides. Nothing crowds the seal.

§26.6 **Misuse (forbidden):** stretching or distorting; rotating (the star's orientation is fixed); recolouring; adding effects (shadow, glow, gradient); placing on a busy image without sufficient contrast; using it as a decorative texture or repeating pattern; sub-branding a department with a modified seal (§7.4).

## Chapter 27 — Icons

§27.1 **System.** Line icons on a 24-unit grid, uniform stroke **1.4 units**, round caps and joins, no fills except tiny functional dots. Icons inherit text colour (§16.2) and never carry colour of their own.

§27.2 **The set** (canonical): Onward (arrow-right), Descend (arrow-down / scroll), Close, Menu, Register (search), Apothecary (mortar & pestle), Academy (open book), Journeys (path), Khatim (the eight-point mark), Botanical (leaf). New icons are drawn to this grammar or not added.

§27.3 **Usage.** Icons are used sparingly, only where a word will not do. They never replace a label where a label reads clearly. Every functional icon has an accessible name (§40.7). Decorative icons are hidden from assistive technology.

§27.4 **Forbidden:** filled or two-tone icons; emoji; icon families from third-party trend packs; icons used purely to decorate a heading.

## Chapter 28 — Buttons & Links

§28.1 **The quiet link — the primary affordance.** The institution's default action is the quiet text-link: Inter Nav token, letterspaced, with a 1px Gilt underline offset ~0.35rem.
- *States:* rest (Gilt underline); hover/focus (colour warms to Gilt, underline animates in over 0.45s); focus-visible (Gilt ring, §40.4). Visited state is unchanged — the institution does not exploit memory.
- On myrtle: text Bone, underline Gilt Soft.

§28.2 **The "go" link.** A directional link (e.g. "Enter the Academy →"): on hover, the arrow advances (gap widens ~4px) over 0.45s. A restrained forward gesture, never a colour change.

§28.3 **The solid action — rationed.** A single solid button is permitted in exactly three committed contexts and nowhere else: **Add to the counter** (Apothecary), **Enrol** (Academy), **Request a consultation** (Consultations).
- *Anatomy:* Myrtle ground, 1px Myrtle border, Bone label (Inter Nav), padding ~0.95rem × 1.9rem, radius 0.
- *States:* rest (Myrtle); hover/focus (fills Gilt, label stays Bone); focus-visible (Gilt ring); disabled (transparent ground, Ash label, Rule border — outlined, never greyed-and-shadowed) with an explanation always present.
- There is **one** solid button in any view, appearing only after the knowledge and provenance that must precede a commitment (§25.2 rule 4).

§28.4 **Forbidden:** pill shapes; any corner radius; drop shadows or elevation; gradient fills; icon-plus-label "primary CTA" clusters; more than one solid button per view; any button bearing an urgency or scarcity word (§10.2).

## Chapter 29 — Cards & Rows

§29.1 **Preference.** The institution prefers the **ruled row of a bibliography** to the boxed card of a shop. Cards are used only where a set of true peers must be shown together; otherwise, ruled rows.

§29.2 **Card anatomy.** Where used: 1px Rule border, radius 0, no shadow, generous internal padding (≥s4). Cards do not float; depth is the hairline and the ground only.

§29.3 **Department card.** Numeral (I/II/III, real structure) · name (Title) · role-line (Micro) · one sentence (Body) · a "go" link at the foot. Three, equal, in fixed order.

§29.4 **Listing row.** Title (Title token) left; provenance (grade · source, Micro) right; 1px Rule beneath. The grade and source appear *in the row itself* — provenance is visible before the item is opened.

§29.5 **Pathway bridge.** A ruled cross-reference block: "See also — taught in the Academy → · used on the [name] retreat →." Styled as a scholar's cross-reference. **It is never styled or worded as commercial cross-sell** ("you may also like," "customers also bought" are forbidden).

§29.6 **Specimen card** — see §32.

§29.7 **Covenant list & grave block** — ruled promise-lines / a Display-L line on myrtle, rationed to gravity (§14.3, §21.7).

## Chapter 30 — Forms

§30.1 **Posture.** Forms are calm, generous, and forgiving — designed for a person who may be unwell (§3.5). Labels are serif and human; the form asks only what the institution truly needs (§8.4 restraint).

§30.2 **Field anatomy.** Serif label (Body) above; optional Micro hint; input with 1px Rule border, radius 0, Bone ground, ~0.85rem × 1rem padding, Body-size text. Target height ≥44px (§40.5).

§30.3 **States.** Rest (Rule border); focus (Gilt border + a 2px Gilt underline drawn on the inner edge — not a glow); filled (unchanged); error (Oxblood border + message, §30.6); disabled (only with a stated reason).

§30.4 **Multi-step.** Long intakes (Consultations) are broken into calm, numbered steps (numbering legitimate — a true sequence). Progress is shown soberly; no step is hidden to inflate a sense of speed.

§30.5 **Labels & binding.** Every field has a real, programmatically associated label. Placeholders are never used as labels. Required fields are marked in words, not by colour alone.

§30.6 **Validation.** Inline, on blur or submit, in the institution's voice: it explains and guides, never scolds ("We'll need a way to reach you — an address like name@example.com"). Error text is Oxblood, tied to its field for assistive technology. **No red flood, no "Error: invalid," no exclamation.** Success is confirmed plainly (§38.3).

§30.7 **Forbidden:** required fields the institution does not need; pre-checked opt-ins; consent walls that punish refusal; forced account creation for acquisition (guest acquisition is honoured, §39.4).

## Chapter 31 — Navigation

§31.1 **Masthead** (the corridor). Left: seal (small) + wordmark (Cormorant, letterspaced) + Arabic sub-line *al-ṭibb al-nabawī*; the seal/wordmark return home. Right: the three departments in fixed order, then a hairline divider, then **Consultations** in Gilt (set apart because clinical care is a different *kind* of act). Solid Bone ground (opaque, never blurred), 1px Rule beneath, sticky.
- *No basket, no account, no search in the masthead by default* — the masthead sells nothing and counts nothing.
- *Tablet:* links wrap to a second line, still inline. *Mobile:* links collapse behind "Menu" into a full-height Myrtle panel (an interior stepped into), with focus trap, Esc to close, focus returned (§40.6).

§31.2 **Footer / colophon.** Myrtle-deep ground. Identity block (seal, wordmark, Arabic name, one-line charter) + link columns: Departments · The Institution (Charter, Covenant, On Authenticity, Sources) · Correspond (Enquiries, For Practitioners, The Register). Base line: Roman-numeral date + the maxim "Healing is from Allah · the remedy is a means." Column order is constant across breakpoints.

§31.3 **In-page navigation.** Contents (sticky rail on desktop → top disclosure on mobile); running head + folio; "see also" pathways; breadcrumb in the institution's terms (*The Apothecary / [remedy]*).

§31.4 **The Register (search).** A single serif search line in its own reading room, never in global chrome. Placeholder: "Search the institution's texts, remedies, and sources." Results are ruled rows carrying grade/source and department tag.

§31.5 **Forbidden:** mega-menus; a persistent basket badge counting items; a gamified "my account" dashboard; sticky pop-up navigation; notification nags.

## Chapter 32 — The Attestation Apparatus *(the signature system)*

§32.1 **Purpose.** The apparatus makes the tradition's sourcing discipline visible and repeatable. It is the single element that communicates scholarship, authenticity, and clinical rigour at once, and it is the institution's design signature.

§32.2 **The specimen (block form).** A Leaf-ground container, 1px Rule border, a small "SPECIMEN" tab on the top border. Contents: the statement (Title/Display), transliteration (italic Body), a 1px Rule, then the apparatus rows (Grade · Source · Standing) as a description list (Micro keys, Body values). The Grade carries a small Gilt dot **and** the grade word (never colour alone, §38.4).

§32.3 **The source-mark (inline form).** A Micro siglum (gilt superscript) beside an in-line claim. On hover/focus/tap it reveals the source — in the margin (desktop), as a numbered footnote (tablet), or expanded beneath the paragraph (mobile). Every source-mark is keyboard-reachable and touch-reachable; provenance is never hover-only (§40.8).

§32.4 **Grade badges** within reading text use the grade word in Micro caps, optionally with the Gilt dot. The three grades and their meanings are fixed (§12.1).

§32.5 **The static rule.** The apparatus **never collapses or toggles.** An attestation that can be dismissed reads as optional; the institution's provenance is not optional. The apparatus is always open, always inline, always adjacent to the claim it supports.

§32.6 **Relocation, not removal.** Across breakpoints the apparatus relocates (margin → footnote → inline-expand) but is never removed to save space (§25.1 rule 3). This is the hardest and most important responsive commitment in the manual.

# PART EIGHT — IMAGERY: PHOTOGRAPHY, ILLUSTRATION & ORNAMENT
*Pages 117–128*

Photography is the *only* sanctioned difference between departments (§7.3). It therefore carries a heavy share of the institution's character and is specified as tightly as type.

## Chapter 33 — Photography

§33.1 **The grammar** (one, across all three subject-worlds):
- Natural light only; the hours of dawn and dusk favoured. No hard studio strobe aesthetics, no coloured gels.
- Stillness — the patience of the still life. Subjects are composed, not caught.
- The subject given deep margin; never cropped to fill the frame. Space around the object is the reverence.
- Matter treated with the dignity of a relic; the human, where present, treated modestly and never as spectacle.
- Muted, true colour; the institution's tonal temperature. No HDR, no saturation-boost, no filters.

§33.2 **The three subject-worlds** (differentiation carried here, not by palette):

| Department | Subject-world | Temperature | Canonical ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Apothecary | Still-life vitrine — the remedy as object (honey, black seed, oil, herb) | Warm | 4:5 |
| The Academy | Manuscript & ink — text, pen, page, shelf | Cool, archival | 4:5 |
| Sacred Journeys | Landscape & place — light, horizon, stone, road | Atmospheric | 16:9 |

§33.3 **Do (binding art direction):** single clear subject; generous negative space; visible texture; one soft directional light; a considered, calm composition; images that could sit unchanged for decades.

§33.4 **Never:** lifestyle vitality or wellness performance; stock smiles; hands-holding-product; travel-brochure saturation; sterile white-background e-commerce shots (unless a deliberate archival treatment); auto-playing video; motion for its own sake; any image implying a benefit the copy may not claim (§10.3).

§33.5 **The photography brief template** (used to commission every shoot): subject; department & subject-world; ratio; light (time of day, direction); mood (one word, from the grammar); margin (how much space around subject); what it must *not* imply. No shoot proceeds without a brief passing the Standard (§4).

## Chapter 34 — Illustration & Ornament

§34.1 **Stance.** Where illustration is needed, it comes from the **manuscript and the herbarium** — geometric ornament and botanical line drawing — never the flat cartoon vocabulary of the contemporary brand.

§34.2 **Vocabulary:** the eight-point *khatim* as seal and section ornament; botanical line studies of the *materia medica* (black seed, olive, senna, honeycomb) to accompany monographs; hairline rule-ornaments to open or close a leaf. All drawn in a single thin Gilt stroke — engraved marks, not filled graphics.

§34.3 **Forbidden:** mascots; isometric or "corporate memphis" scenes; gradient spot-illustration; character illustration of any kind; illustration used to make a serious subject "friendly."

## Chapter 35 — Asset Production & Specification

§35.1 **Formats.** Photography delivered as high-resolution originals plus responsive derivatives; served in a modern, well-supported format with broad fallbacks; never dependent on a single proprietary format. Vector marks (seal, icons, ornament) delivered as clean, minimal vector with the geometry of §26 preserved.

§35.2 **Resolution & responsive sets.** Each image is produced at sufficient resolution for the largest placement at 2× density, and derived down for smaller viewports. Images are never upscaled. Object images retain their deep margin at every size (never re-cropped to fill on mobile, §33.1).

§35.3 **Alt-text standard.** Every content image of the *materia medica* or a place carries descriptive alt text naming the subject plainly (e.g. "Black seeds in a shallow dish, side-lit"). Ornamental marks and the seal are marked decorative and hidden from assistive technology (§40.7). Alt text never repeats a health claim.

§35.4 **Naming & governance.** Assets are named by subject and department, versioned, and stored in one managed library. No image enters the product outside the library. Licences and model/property releases are recorded for every asset and retained.

§35.5 **Longevity.** Because assets must serve for decades, the institution prefers timeless subjects and treatments (§33) and commissions originals it owns over licensed stock it does not. Trend-bound imagery is refused at commission, not at publication.

# PART NINE — MOTION

## Chapter 36 — Doctrine & Tokens

§36.1 **Doctrine.** Motion serves calm and comprehension. It never manufactures urgency, never seizes the eye, never entertains. The page unfolds at the reader's walking pace; nothing bounces, parallaxes, jumps, or auto-plays.

§36.2 **Tokens** (fixed):

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Easing (standard) | cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.2,1) | All transitions — a settled deceleration |
| Duration — quick | 0.30s | Page cross-fade, small acknowledgements |
| Duration — base | 0.45s | Link underline, nav slide, hover |
| Duration — slow | 1.10s | Scroll reveal (18px rise + fade) |

§36.3 — No motion uses a bounce, elastic, or overshoot easing. No duration exceeds ~1.2s. No animation loops indefinitely except the loader's gentle breathing (§38.2), which is itself paused under reduced motion.

## Chapter 37 — The Motion Catalogue

§37.1 — Every sanctioned motion, with trigger and behaviour. Nothing outside this catalogue is added without amendment (§41).

| Motion | Trigger | Behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| Threshold load sequence | First paint of home | Bismillah → seal → declaration → subline → links, staggered fade-up (once) |
| Scroll reveal | Section enters viewport | 18px rise + fade, ~1.1s, once |
| Link underline | Hover / focus | Gilt underline draws in, ~0.45s |
| "Go" link advance | Hover / focus | Arrow gap widens ~4px |
| Nav slide-in (mobile) | Menu | Myrtle panel eases from right, 0.5s; focus moves in; Esc/overlay closes; focus returns |
| Source-mark expand | Hover / focus / tap | Source note reveals in margin / footnote / beneath paragraph |
| Reading progress | Scroll (long text) | A hairline fills along the top edge; folio updates |
| Add-to-counter | Solid action | Quiet inline confirmation line; no pop-up cart, no upsell |
| Page transition | Navigation | Brief cross-fade (~0.3s); chrome persists |
| Loader | Waiting | Faint seal breathes 25%→70% opacity over 3s |

§37.2 **Reduced motion.** Under `prefers-reduced-motion`, all reveals resolve instantly (content simply present), the loader rests at ~50% opacity, and the threshold sequence appears at once. **No content ever depends on motion to be readable or reachable** (§40.6).

§37.3 **Forbidden motion:** parallax; scroll-jacking / scroll-hijacked pinning; auto-advancing carousels; kinetic or animated type; auto-playing video or sound; hover animations that move layout; confetti or celebratory bursts; any motion whose purpose is to create urgency or delight rather than to orient.

# PART TEN — STATES, PATTERNS & COMMERCE
*Pages 135–140*

## Chapter 38 — System States

§38.1 **Principle.** Emptiness and failure are moments for direction, not mood. States speak in the institution's voice (§9), never a person's, never a mascot's; they explain and guide, and never blame the reader.

§38.2 **Loading.** A faint, breathing seal (§37.1) with a Micro label "One moment." No spinner, no shimmer-skeleton, no progress theatre. Long reading pages stream content progressively; the reader begins reading the moment the first paragraph is ready.

§38.3 **Success.** Confirmed plainly. Form submitted: "Your message is received. We answer as an institution answers — considered, and in time." Acquisition added: a quiet inline line, no pop-up.

§38.4 **State colour.** States are carried by the institution's palette and by **words**, not by traffic-light colour. There is no "success green" or "alert red"; genuine error uses Oxblood *and* explanatory words (§13.3, §16.3). Meaning is never conveyed by colour alone (§40.3).

§38.5 **The canonical strings** (binding copy):

| Situation | The string |
|---|---|
| Empty required field (name) | We'll need your name to reply. |
| Invalid email | We'll need a way to reach you — an address like name@example.com. |
| No search results | The Register holds no entry under that term. Try a broader one, search within a single department, or write to us and we will look ourselves. |
| 404 | This leaf is not in the institution. Return to the threshold, or search the Register. |
| Empty basket | The counter is empty. The cabinet is here. |
| Remedy unavailable | This remedy is not in stock at present. Write to us to be told when it returns. |
| Payment declined | That payment did not go through, and nothing was charged. You may try again, or write to us. |
| System error | Something on our side has faltered — nothing you did is at fault. Please try again shortly. |

§38.6 **Forbidden state copy:** "Oops," emoji, "Error 404," "Invalid input," "Please fill out this field," "You forgot something," "Hurry, almost gone."

## Chapter 39 — Commerce & Ethics

§39.1 **The Counter.** The Apothecary's basket is *the Counter*; items are *your selection*; each carries its source-mark to the basket (§8.3) — a remedy is never an anonymous SKU. Acquisition is a calm, numbered sequence: details → delivery → payment → confirm.

§39.2 **Placement.** The single solid action and the price appear *beneath* the knowledge and provenance on every commerce surface (§25.1 rule 4). The person reaches the price only after tradition, benefit, honest limits, and provenance.

§39.3 **Payment.** Card and credential entry is performed by the person in the payment provider's own secure interface; the institution's UI never captures raw payment credentials. Delivery and fees are stated plainly with no surprise charges (*gharar* forbidden).

§39.4 **Honoured mercies:** guest acquisition (no forced account); clear, plain pricing; disclosed provenance; free essentials in the Academy (§8, Constitution).

§39.5 **The forbidden patterns (dark patterns — absolute prohibition).** The institution never uses: countdown timers; stock-scarcity counters ("only N left," "12 people viewing"); manufactured urgency of any kind; exit-intent pop-ups; pre-checked add-ons or opt-ins; "confirm-shaming" decline language; roach-motel flows (easy in, hard out); forced continuity / hidden auto-renewal; drip-revealed fees; fake social proof; cookie walls that punish refusal; any nag, badge, or notification designed to compel return. A proposal containing any of these is rejected on sight; no metric justifies it (§4.2).

# PART ELEVEN — ACCESSIBILITY, QUALITY & GOVERNANCE

## Chapter 40 — The Accessibility Standard

§40.1 **Posture.** Access is *raḥma*. The institution owes its dignity to every reader — including the elderly, the unwell, and those who navigate by keyboard or screen reader. Where a choice must be made between a striking effect and an accessible one, the institution chooses access, without exception it would wish to defend in a hundred years.

§40.2 **Conformance.** WCAG 2.1 **AA is the floor**; AAA is met wherever the palette and type allow.

§40.3 **Colour independence.** No information is conveyed by colour alone. Grades carry a word, not only a dot; errors carry text, not only Oxblood; links are underlined, not merely coloured.

§40.4 **Focus.** Every interactive element shows a visible Gilt focus ring, offset; focus order follows reading order; focus is never removed or suppressed.

§40.5 **Targets.** Interactive targets are ≥44×44px. Spacing prevents accidental activation.

§40.6 **Keyboard & motion.** Full keyboard operability, including the mobile nav (focus trap, Esc to close, focus returned). All motion honours `prefers-reduced-motion`; nothing conveys meaning by motion alone; nothing is reachable only by hover (§32.3, §37.2).

§40.7 **Semantics & names.** Semantic headings and landmarks throughout; one H1 per page; forms use real associated labels; functional icons and controls have accessible names; the seal and ornament are hidden from assistive technology; the *materia medica* photography carries descriptive alt text (§35.3).

§40.8 **Provenance for all.** Every source-mark is reachable by keyboard and touch; the apparatus is present in the DOM, not injected only on hover, so assistive technology encounters the sourcing (§32).

§40.9 **Privacy & data.** Personal or sensitive data is never placed in a URL; consent is honest and reversible; the most privacy-preserving option is the default on any consent choice; user data is treated as a sacred trust and never sold or misused (Constitution, *amāna*).

§40.10 **Language & RTL.** The document language is declared; Arabic runs are marked Arabic and right-to-left; mixed-direction lines are isolated so punctuation renders correctly (§20.4).

## Chapter 41 — Build Acceptance & Governance

§41.1 **Definition of done (build acceptance).** No screen ships until it satisfies, and a reviewer has verified, all of the following:

1. Every section passes the Institutional Standard (§4).
2. Order of sections matches the responsive law (§25.1 rule 2) at all three breakpoints.
3. The attestation apparatus is present and relocates correctly at every breakpoint; no claim lacks a source (§32, §8.4).
4. Commerce and committed actions sit beneath knowledge (§25.1 rule 4); at most one solid button per view (§28.3).
5. Chrome (masthead, footer, seal, type, grid, voice) is identical to specification (§7.2, §31).
6. Contrast meets §15 for every text/ground pairing actually used; gilt-text restriction respected (§15.4).
7. Full keyboard path, visible focus, ≥44px targets, reduced-motion behaviour verified (§40).
8. Copy contains no refused word (§10.2) and no health claim (§10.3); all states use the canonical strings (§38.5).
9. No forbidden trend (Appendix A) or dark pattern (§39.5) is present.
10. Arabic and citations are set to standard (§12, §20).

§41.2 **Review.** Every release is reviewed against §41.1 by a designer and an editor who did not build it. A failed criterion blocks release; it is fixed, not waived.

§41.3 **Amendment process.** This manual is amended only in the open: a proposal states the clause to change, the reason, and its effect; it is checked against the Constitution and the Standard; if adopted, it is dated and recorded in the Decision Record (Appendix E), and the change is applied to the manual first, then the product. The foundations in §0.1 are not amendable.

§41.4 **Versioning.** The manual carries an edition number and date. The product states which edition it conforms to. Superseded editions are retained, so the institution's design history is inheritable and auditable.

§41.5 **Custodianship.** One named custodian holds the manual and convenes amendments. The custodianship is an *amāna*: the holder maintains the manual for successors, not for themselves, and hands it on intact.

**The institution uses instead:** hairline rules & ground changes for depth; solid opaque grounds; square corners; one rationed gilt; still composition at the reader's pace; reverent still photography; geometric & botanical line ornament; ruled columns, quiet links, folios; a faint still seal; in-voice states.

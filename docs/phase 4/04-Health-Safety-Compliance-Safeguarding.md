# Chapter 04 · Health, Safety, Compliance & Safeguarding

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Facilities & FOH Lead + Clinical Lead (clinical sections) · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual, and on any incident or regulatory change
> *Critical note:* Safety and legal compliance are **non-negotiable** and sit above all commercial considerations (Handbook order of authority, Ch 00). This chapter sets our operational standard; the institution retains qualified health-and-safety, clinical-governance, food-safety, safeguarding, and legal/regulatory professionals whose guidance is binding and whose specifics govern where they differ from this text. **This is operational scaffolding, not professional or legal advice.**

---

## 4.1 The safety culture

Everyone is responsible for safety — theirs, their colleagues', and every guest's. Two habits define us:

- **Stop the line.** Anyone, at any level, may halt any activity they believe is unsafe. Doing so is always right, never penalised. This is the physical twin of the slow lane.
- **Report everything.** Near-misses are reported like incidents, because they are the warnings we get for free.

## 4.2 The compliance map

The institution operates a live compliance register (owned by the Standards Council, Chapter 12) covering at least:

- **Health & safety** — premises risk assessments, fire, first aid, equipment, manual handling.
- **Clinical governance** — for Consultations and Hijama (§4.6–4.7).
- **Special treatment / local-authority licensing** — cupping/therapeutic treatments commonly require registration or a licence from the local authority; **this is confirmed and held before any treatment is offered.**
- **Food safety & hygiene** — the dispensary handles food-grade goods (saffron, honey, etc.); registration and food-hygiene standards apply (§4.8).
- **Waste** — general, recycling, and **clinical/hazardous waste** via a licensed carrier (§4.9).
- **Data protection** — guest and patient data (§4.10, Chapter 11).
- **Safeguarding** — children and vulnerable adults (§4.11).
- **Insurance** — public liability, professional/clinical indemnity, employer's liability, travel/duty-of-care for Journeys, product liability.
- **Consumer, advertising, and labelling** — honest claims (Blueprint governance), product labelling, distance-selling rights.

Each register entry: obligation, owner, evidence held, renewal/review date. Nothing regulated goes live without its register entry green.

## 4.3 Risk assessments

- Every activity and zone has a **written risk assessment**: hazards, who might be harmed, existing controls, further action, review date.
- Assessments are reviewed at least annually, after any incident, and when anything changes (new equipment, new treatment, new space).
- Specific assessments required for: clinic/Hijama, dispensary/food handling, photography studio (lighting/electrical), manual handling (deliveries/stock), lone working, and events/teaching.

## 4.4 Fire safety

- Fire risk assessment maintained and current.
- Exits and routes kept clear at all times (part of every opening/closing sweep, Chapter 03).
- Detection and extinguishers serviced on schedule; never overdue.
- **Everyone knows** the evacuation route, assembly point, and alarm on day one (onboarding, Chapter 02).
- Fire drills conducted periodically and logged.
- Fire marshals appointed and trained.

## 4.5 First aid & medical emergencies

- Trained first-aiders on site during operating hours; names posted.
- First-aid kits stocked and checked; locations known to all.
- **Emergency procedure** (posted in every zone): make safe → call emergency services if needed → first aid by trained person → do not exceed your competence → record.
- For clinical areas, additional emergency protocols apply (§4.7).
- Any guest medical event is recorded (§4.12) and reviewed.

## 4.6 Infection prevention & control (IPC) — foundational standard

IPC is the backbone of a safe clinic and dispensary. **This section is safety-critical and non-optional.**

- **Hand hygiene:** the single most important control. Handwashing stations and sanitiser available; hand-hygiene protocol trained and enforced for anyone in clinical or food-handling zones.
- **Personal protective equipment (PPE):** appropriate PPE for the task, correctly used and changed.
- **Clean/dirty separation:** clear separation of clean and used items; no cross-contamination between guests, ever.
- **Single-use where required:** items that contact blood or broken skin are single-use and safely disposed; nothing that should be single-use is ever reused.
- **Cleaning and decontamination:** clinical surfaces and any reusable equipment are cleaned/decontaminated to the required standard between every guest.
- **Immunisation and health of staff:** clinical staff follow occupational-health guidance appropriate to their exposure; unwell staff do not work in clinical or food zones.
- IPC standards are set with the Clinical Lead in line with recognised professional guidance and confirmed with clinical-governance counsel.

## 4.7 Clinical safety — Consultations & Hijama (governance level)

Detailed clinical SOPs are in Chapter 06. At the safety level:

- **Licensed and trained only.** Hijama and clinical treatments are performed **only** by appropriately trained, and where required licensed/registered, practitioners, within scope.
- **Informed consent** is obtained and recorded before any treatment.
- **Screening and contraindications** are checked; red-flag symptoms are referred to medical care — we do not treat outside our competence.
- **Sharps and blood safety:** strict sharps handling, safe disposal in approved containers, and a sharps-injury protocol (immediate first aid + occupational-health follow-up + recording).
- **Emergency readiness:** a clear protocol for a guest reaction or collapse (make safe, first aid, emergency services, record).
- **Not a substitute for medical care:** stated to every guest; we complement, never replace, professional healthcare, and we never discourage a guest from seeking it.

## 4.8 Food safety & hygiene (the dispensary)

Because we handle food-grade products:

- Food-business registration and hygiene standards maintained; a food-safety management approach (hazard-based) documented.
- **Temperature control and logging** for anything requiring it (§Chapter 05).
- Storage protects against contamination, pests, light, and spoilage; stock rotation (first-expiry-first-out).
- **Allergen management:** allergen information accurate and controlled (this is both a safety and a legal duty).
- Personal hygiene for food handlers; unwell handlers excluded.
- Cleaning schedules for food-contact areas.

## 4.9 Waste management

- **General and recycling** handled responsibly.
- **Clinical/hazardous waste** (anything blood-contaminated, sharps) is segregated at source, stored in approved containers in a secure area, and collected by a **licensed clinical-waste carrier** with documentation retained (duty of care applies to waste too).
- Sharps containers are never overfilled and are sealed/replaced per standard.

## 4.10 Data protection & confidentiality

- Guest and **patient data are confidential** and handled under data-protection law (lawful basis, minimal collection, secure storage, defined retention, guest rights).
- **Physical privacy:** clinical records, releases, and PII are locked away; clean-desk rule at close (Chapter 03).
- **Digital privacy:** never in URLs/filenames; role-based access; encryption and secure systems (Chapter 11).
- **Special-category (health) data** receives the highest protection.
- Breaches are reported immediately per the breach procedure and to the regulator where required.

## 4.11 Safeguarding

- The institution protects children and vulnerable adults who interact with it (clinic, courses, journeys).
- **A designated safeguarding lead** is appointed; everyone knows who and how to raise a concern.
- Relevant roles complete background checks before guest contact (Chapter 02).
- **Chaperoning and appropriate practice** in clinical settings (Chapter 06), including sensitivity to gender — same-gender practitioner/chaperone arrangements offered as standard where appropriate.
- Concerns are acted on promptly, recorded, and escalated to the safeguarding lead and, where required, external authorities. Safeguarding overrides confidentiality when someone is at risk.

## 4.12 Incident & accident reporting SOP

**Purpose:** every incident becomes a lesson, not a repeat.
**Trigger:** any accident, near-miss, injury, guest medical event, security incident, data breach, or safeguarding concern.

1. **Make safe** — protect people first.
2. **Respond** — first aid / emergency services / containment as needed.
3. **Record** — in the incident log: what, when, who, what was done. For reportable incidents, the required external report is made within the legal timeframe.
4. **Report up** — to the relevant lead and, for serious matters, the Standards Council.
5. **Review** — root cause identified; controls updated; risk assessment revised; the fix is shared so it doesn't recur.

## 4.13 Acceptance criteria (HSE & Safeguarding)

- [ ] Live compliance register; nothing regulated goes live without a green entry.
- [ ] Written, current risk assessments for every activity and zone.
- [ ] Fire, first-aid, and emergency arrangements in place; everyone briefed on day one.
- [ ] IPC standards enforced in all clinical and food zones; single-use respected; clean/dirty separated.
- [ ] Hijama/clinical treatment only by trained (and where required licensed) practitioners, with consent, screening, sharps and emergency protocols.
- [ ] Food-safety and allergen management maintained in the dispensary.
- [ ] Clinical waste segregated and removed by a licensed carrier with documentation.
- [ ] Data protection and confidentiality upheld physically and digitally; breaches reported.
- [ ] Safeguarding lead appointed; checks, chaperoning, and escalation in place.
- [ ] Every incident recorded, reported, and reviewed to root cause.

# Chapter 08 · Sacred Journeys: Office Operations & Duty of Care

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Director of Journeys · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual, and before every journey
> *Companion:* Blueprint Doc 07 governs the full journey lifecycle. This chapter governs the **HQ office operations** behind it — administration, screening handling, documentation, and duty-of-care logistics.

---

## 8.1 The first principle, restated

A journey is a **duty of care before it is a product.** When safety and commerce conflict, safety wins without discussion (Blueprint Doc 07). Everyone on the Journeys team holds that principle before any commercial target.

## 8.2 Enquiry & registration handling SOP

- A journey is **joined through conversation**, not a cart. Enquiries are answered warmly, personally, and honestly about the journey's nature and demands.
- Register interest, capture initial details, and set expectations (physical demands, spiritual intent, group nature).
- No deposit is taken before screening (§8.3) — we never let someone commit to a journey we haven't confirmed is safe for them.

## 8.3 Screening administration SOP

Screening is a **protection, not a formality** — administer it with care and rigour.

1. Issue the **suitability + health screening** (designed with the Clinical Board, Blueprint Doc 07).
2. Collect responses into **restricted, secure storage** (health data = special-category PII; Chapter 04.10, Chapter 11) — never in email bodies, filenames, or URLs.
3. Route health information to the **clinical reviewer** for a decision; do not make clinical judgements at the admin desk.
4. Record the outcome: **cleared / cleared-with-accommodation / screened-out.**
5. **Communicate the outcome with rifq** (Blueprint Doc 07): screen-outs are handled personally, privately, and with an alternative offered — never a cold rejection.

## 8.4 Payments & documentation SOP

- **Deposit/balance** taken only after clearance; refund/transfer terms shown before payment (Blueprint Doc 07).
- **Documentation collected securely:** travel documents, health information, emergency contacts, and informed-consent forms — all as restricted PII, controlled access, retained per legal requirement, then securely disposed.
- A **documentation checklist per guest** ensures nothing is missing before departure.
- Payment records kept; financial handling follows the institution's finance controls (no PII in insecure channels).

## 8.5 Pre-departure preparation SOP

The office runs a staged **pre-journey sequence** (Blueprint Doc 07) — thorough, because preparation is both safety and meaning:

- [ ] Welcome + what the journey asks of you
- [ ] **Educational material** (reviewed, scholarly — Blueprint Doc 02) delivered in a considered rhythm
- [ ] Logistics: travel details, timings, group information
- [ ] **Packing guidance** appropriate to destination and demands
- [ ] Final pre-departure: confirmations, named contact, emergency-protocol summary, arrival instructions

## 8.6 Duty-of-care logistics SOP

**Before every journey (the office confirms and holds evidence of):**
- [ ] Risk assessment refreshed for this journey
- [ ] **Emergency protocol** documented and staff briefed (medical access mapped, escalation path clear, next-of-kin reachable)
- [ ] **Insurance** in force (travel, medical, liability, and travel-organiser obligations confirmed with counsel)
- [ ] **Safeguarding** arrangements and a named safeguarding lead for the journey (Chapter 04.11)
- [ ] Staffing confirmed with defined responsibilities
- [ ] Every guest cleared, documented, and consented
- [ ] Named contact reachable throughout travel

**During travel** (coordinated from HQ where relevant): named contact live, incidents managed by protocol and logged, families reachable.

**After:** incident review (Director + Clinical Board where health-related); lessons written into the next journey's dossier.

## 8.7 Return, follow-up & community SOP

- **Welcome-home** communication is **pastoral and upsell-free** (Blueprint Doc 07) — a journey's end is sacred, not a sales moment.
- Reflective material offered; an invitation (not a demand) to share.
- Genuine **follow-up** check-in.
- Guest enrolled into the **journey's alumni fellowship** — a lasting bond and future journeys.
- Feedback collected with care; themes feed continuous improvement.

## 8.8 Records & confidentiality

- Screening, health data, documentation, consent, payments, and incident logs are retained securely per legal requirement and then disposed.
- Health and travel data are **special-category / sensitive PII** — highest protection, controlled access, never in insecure channels (Chapter 04.10, Chapter 11).

## 8.9 Acceptance criteria (Sacred Journeys office)

- [ ] No deposit before clearance; two-part screening administered for every applicant.
- [ ] Health data handled as restricted PII throughout; clinical decisions made by clinical reviewers, not admin.
- [ ] Screen-outs communicated personally with an alternative; terms shown before payment.
- [ ] Documentation checklist complete per guest before departure.
- [ ] Duty-of-care pack (risk assessment, emergency protocol, insurance, safeguarding, staffing, named contact) confirmed before every journey.
- [ ] Return communication is pastoral; follow-up and community enrolment happen.
- [ ] All records retained securely and disposed per policy.

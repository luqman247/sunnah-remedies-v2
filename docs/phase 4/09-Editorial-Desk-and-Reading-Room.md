# Chapter 09 · The Editorial Desk & Reading Room

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Editor-in-Chief · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual
> *Companion:* Blueprint Doc 02 is the full Editorial Manual (content lifecycle, review chains, verification, audit). This chapter is the **desk-level SOP** — how an editor works day to day, and how the reading room runs.

---

## 9.1 The editor's creed (at the desk)

You are a curator, not a copywriter. You write the museum label, not the advert. Three habits govern every piece:

1. **Publish nothing you cannot source.** Provenance before polish.
2. **Distinguish the four layers, always** (Chapter 01) — established / interpreted / traditional / ours.
3. **Slow is a feature.** Pull anything into the slow lane the moment scholarship or safety is in question.

## 9.2 The editorial desk SOP

Follows the nine-stage lifecycle (Blueprint Doc 02). At the desk:

1. **Take the brief** — purpose, audience, type, required reviews, target date.
2. **Draft to house style** (§9.4), tagging each claim's layer as you write.
3. **Source and verify** — trace every factual and religious claim to a source *before* review begins. Hadith graded and attributed; Qur'an verified; health claims evidence-matched. Unsourceable claims are **cut, not softened.**
4. **Send through the review chain** — Scholarly Board (anything touching the tradition), Clinical Board (anything touching the body), Editorial (everything). Nothing publishes without the required sign-offs.
5. **Complete the provenance record** (Blueprint Doc 02 §D) — no record, no publish.
6. **Publish to standard**, set the next-review date, enter the audit schedule.
7. **Version** every change; substantive edits to verified content **re-trigger** the relevant Board.

## 9.3 The verification desk (research)

- Every claim gets a source before it is reviewed. This is the desk's discipline, not an optional extra.
- **The Ibn al-Qayyim rule:** a quote attributed to a scholar that cannot be traced to a verified source is treated as an **unverified modern paraphrase** — removed, or explicitly flagged as such. It is never laundered into "established." (This precedent applies to any attribution.)
- Hadith are cited with collection, number, and grading; weak/fabricated narrations are never presented as sound and, if referenced at all, are clearly labelled.

## 9.4 Writing standard (desk reference)

- **One institution, one voice.** Read it aloud — does it sound like the last thing we published?
- **Restraint:** no superlatives, no urgency, no clickbait. The provenance carries the authority; the adjective only weakens it. Cut what can be cut.
- **Register:** transactional copy quiet and precise; pastoral copy warm and unhurried; never mixed.
- **Honesty over persuasion** always.
- **Safety placement:** health content carries the "not a substitute for medical advice" line where the reader decides, not buried in a policy page.
- **Consistency:** house transliteration standard applied throughout.

## 9.5 Content audit SOP

- **Quarterly sample + annual full** audit (Blueprint Doc 02 §G) checks: broken/dead citations and links, claims that have drifted from source, missing/outdated safety lines, layer-blurring crept in through edits, stale product/price references, voice/transliteration consistency.
- Findings feed the review queue; a failed item goes to the slow lane until fixed.

## 9.6 The Reading Room / Library operations

The reading room is the physical Knowledge Library — a quiet, dignified space for study, editorial reference, and members.

- **Reset state:** calm, ordered, silent-appropriate; materials shelved and cared for.
- **Reference materials** are catalogued and, where valuable or rare, handled with preservation care and access control (Chapter 04 privacy for any restricted materials).
- **Guest use:** members/guests welcomed for quiet study per the room's use policy; the room's atmosphere is protected (the British Museum reading-room standard).
- **Editorial use:** the desk uses the room for source consultation; borrowed/consulted materials are logged and returned.
- **Digital library** access provided where relevant (Blueprint Doc 09 future digital library).

## 9.7 Records

- Provenance records, review sign-offs, version histories, audit findings, and the content review schedule are all maintained (Blueprint Doc 02).
- Reference-material catalogue and any loan log kept for the reading room.

## 9.8 Acceptance criteria (Editorial & Reading Room)

- [ ] Every claim sourced before review; unsourceable claims cut.
- [ ] Required review chains completed; provenance record complete before publish.
- [ ] Four-layer honesty and the Ibn al-Qayyim rule applied to all tradition-touching content.
- [ ] Writing meets the house voice and restraint standard; safety lines correctly placed.
- [ ] Every piece has a next-review date and is in the audit schedule; audits run and are actioned.
- [ ] Reading room maintained to reset state; reference materials catalogued and cared for.

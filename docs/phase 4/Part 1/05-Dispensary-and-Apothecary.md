# Chapter 05 · The Dispensary & Apothecary

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Head of Apothecary · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual
> *Companion:* Blueprint Doc 05 (product lifecycle, lab verification, launch, recall) governs the product itself. This chapter governs how the **physical dispensary** runs day to day.

---

## 5.1 The dispensary standard

The dispensary is a food-grade preparation and storage space. It runs on three non-negotiables: **provenance you can trace, hygiene you can prove, and honesty you can read on the label.** Every product carries the institution's authority (Blueprint §2), so nothing leaves this room that we couldn't explain, aloud, to the scholar who taught us and the doctor who treats our mother.

## 5.2 Goods-in SOP

**Purpose:** nothing enters our stock unverified.
**Owner:** dispensary duty-holder.

1. **Receive** against the purchase/order; check quantities and condition.
2. **Provenance check:** origin, supplier, batch, and any required certification present (Blueprint Doc 05 sourcing). Reject anything without documentation.
3. **Lab-verification link:** confirm the batch is covered by, or scheduled for, the required verification (authenticity, purity, allergens). **No unverified batch is released to sale.**
4. **Inspect** for damage, tampering, spoilage, pest signs; reject and quarantine anything doubtful.
5. **Record** the batch into stock with received date, quantity, batch/lot, and expiry.
6. **Store** immediately in the correct conditions (§5.3).

**Failure/exception:** anything failing goods-in is quarantined in a marked area, not shelved, and escalated to the Head of Apothecary.

## 5.3 Storage SOP

- **Right conditions per product:** temperature, light, humidity, and air as each product requires (e.g. saffron kept from light and air; honey and perishables per their needs).
- **Temperature-controlled storage is logged** at least at open and close (Chapter 04 food safety); out-of-range readings are acted on immediately and recorded.
- **Separation:** food-grade stock separated from cleaning chemicals and non-food items; allergen-relevant items handled to prevent cross-contamination.
- **Stock rotation:** first-expiry-first-out; expiry dates checked on a schedule.
- **Security:** higher-value stock (e.g. premium saffron) access-controlled.
- **Traceability:** any product on the shelf can be traced back to its batch and verification record.

## 5.4 Stock control SOP

- **Live stock levels** kept accurate and synced with the online system (honest availability — no overselling; Blueprint Doc 05 §J).
- **Reorder points** set per product; replenishment timed to true demand.
- **Cycle counts** on a schedule; a full stock-take periodically. Discrepancies investigated, not just corrected.
- **Expiry management:** approaching-expiry stock flagged; **never sold past integrity** — withdrawn and disposed responsibly, honestly recorded.
- **Demand signals:** frequently-requested or searched-but-out-of-stock items feed the product roadmap (Blueprint Doc 08).

## 5.5 Preparation & handling SOP (food-grade)

- Food-safety hygiene at all times (Chapter 04.8): clean hands, clean surfaces, appropriate PPE, no work while unwell.
- Any decanting, blending, or packing follows a documented method with batch traceability preserved.
- **Allergen control** at every step; allergen information kept accurate to the item.
- Labels applied are the approved, reviewed labels (Blueprint Doc 05 tri-review) — correct product, batch, expiry, allergen, storage, and the required safety/medical-advice line.

## 5.6 Order fulfilment & packing SOP

**Purpose:** what ships is exactly what was ordered, in perfect condition, presented as an artefact.

1. **Pick** against the order; verify item, grade, and batch.
2. **Quality check** the item (condition, expiry, label correct).
3. **Pack** to the institution's presentation standard — protective, considered, brand-consistent (the "unboxing" is part of the guest experience; Blueprint Doc 01).
4. **Include** the correct inserts (care/storage note, any required documentation).
5. **Confirm and dispatch** — trigger the dispatch confirmation (Blueprint Doc 01 email system).
6. **Record** the fulfilment against the order and batch (traceability for any future recall).

**Standard:** zero wrong-item, wrong-batch, or expired dispatches. A packing error is a trust failure, treated seriously.

## 5.7 Returns & complaints (physical handling)

- Returns handled per Blueprint Doc 05 §Returns (fair policy, statutory rights, one-reply resolution).
- **Returned consumables/food-grade items are never re-sold** where safety or integrity could be affected — assessed and, if in doubt, disposed. Guest safety over stock value.
- Quality complaints touching a batch trigger the **recall/hold protocol** (Blueprint Doc 05 §I): hold affected stock, contact affected guests, investigate cause. This is a duty of care, executed fast.

## 5.8 The Apothecary floor (retail experience)

- Display is curated with restraint (Aesop discipline) — considered, uncluttered, honest.
- Product knowledge given to guests is **four-layer honest** (Chapter 01): what tradition establishes vs. our preparation; never inflated.
- **No pressure selling**, no manufactured urgency; the guest is helped, not pushed.
- Health questions beyond general product info are routed to a practitioner — floor staff never improvise clinical advice (Chapter 03 FOH standard).
- Cross-links offered honestly: the Library article on an ingredient, a relevant course or journey (Blueprint Doc 05 §H) — to deepen understanding, not to upsell.

## 5.9 Records & audit

- Batch records, verification links, temperature logs, stock movements, fulfilment records, and returns are all kept and retained (traceability + food-safety + recall readiness).
- Periodic dispensary audit: temperature-log completeness, batch traceability, expiry management, label accuracy, hygiene standard, stock accuracy.

## 5.10 Acceptance criteria (Dispensary & Apothecary)

- [ ] Nothing enters stock without provenance and a verification link; unverified batches never sell.
- [ ] Storage conditions correct and temperature-logged; food/allergen separation maintained.
- [ ] Stock is honest and synced; no overselling; expiry managed; never sold past integrity.
- [ ] Packing produces zero wrong/expired dispatches and an artefact-quality experience.
- [ ] Returned food-grade goods never resold where safety/integrity is in doubt; recall protocol ready.
- [ ] Floor experience is restrained, honest, non-pressuring; clinical questions routed.
- [ ] Full batch-to-dispatch traceability retained; dispensary audited periodically.

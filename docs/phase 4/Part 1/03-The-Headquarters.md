## 3.1 The building as an instrument

The headquarters is not a backdrop; it is the institution made physical. A guest reads our standard in the first three seconds — the light, the quiet, the scent, the order of the space — before anyone speaks. We keep it the way the British Museum keeps a gallery and Aesop keeps a store: **calm, uncluttered, immaculate, and confident.**

Everyone is a custodian of the space. If you see it out of order, you set it right — that is not "someone else's job."

## 3.2 The zones

The HQ is organised into zones, each with its own standard and access level. A zone map is posted at reception and in the back office.

| Zone | Purpose | Access |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Threshold & Reception (Front of House)** | Arrival, welcome, orientation | Public |
| **The Apothecary floor** | Considered display of products; retail experience | Public |

| **Back Office** | Admin, systems, editorial desk | Staff only |

| **Staff areas** | Rest, kitchen, lockers | Staff only |

**Zone standard:** each zone has a "reset state" — how it must look when unattended. Clinical and dispensary zones additionally carry hygiene standards (Chapter 04, 05, 06) that override aesthetics.

## 3.3 Opening SOP (the first hour)

**Purpose:** the institution is safe, clean, and ready before a single guest arrives.
**Owner:** opening duty-holder (rota'd); nothing opens to the public until every box is ticked.

1. **Safety first.** Disarm alarm; check for any overnight incident, leak, or fault; confirm fire exits clear and fire panel normal (Chapter 04).
2. **Environment.** Lights, temperature, ventilation to reset state; scent/ambience as specified.
3. **Cleanliness sweep.** Confirm each public zone is at reset state and clean; clinical and dispensary zones confirmed to their hygiene standard before use.
4. **Clinic readiness** (if clinics today): rooms prepared per Chapter 06; supplies and clinical waste containers checked.
5. **Dispensary readiness** (if fulfilling/serving): fridge/storage temperatures logged (Chapter 05); handwashing stations stocked.
6. **Front of house.** Reception tidy; systems and booking view up; today's appointments, courses, and expected deliveries reviewed.
7. **Team huddle** (5 min): today's guests, courses, clinics, VIPs, deliveries, and anything to watch.
8. **Open** — unlock, welcome state on, ready.

**Opening checklist lives in Chapter 14 and is signed daily.**

## 3.4 Closing SOP (the last hour)

1. **Guests first.** Ensure no guest is rushed; close gently.
2. **Clinical close-down** (Chapter 06): rooms cleaned, waste handled, sharps/clinical waste secured, records filed.
3. **Dispensary close-down** (Chapter 05): stock and temperatures logged, food-grade areas cleaned, perishables secured.
4. **Cash/payments** reconciled if applicable; no cash left in the open (security).
5. **Reset all zones** to reset state for tomorrow's opening.
6. **Security sweep:** windows/doors, no one remaining, no hazards left (heaters, water), confidential material locked away (clean-desk rule — no PII left out).
7. **Systems:** back-office locked, screens locked (Chapter 11).
8. **Alarm and lock.** Log close time and any issues.

**Clean-desk rule:** at close, no guest or patient data, no notes, no keys are left visible anywhere. Privacy is physical too.

## 3.5 Front of House — the welcome standard

Reception is the institution's first sentence. FOH colleagues hold a special amānah.

**The arrival SOP:**
1. **Acknowledge within seconds** — eye contact, a warm, unhurried greeting. No guest stands unnoticed.
2. **Orient** — understand why they've come (browsing, appointment, course, collection, enquiry) and guide them.
3. **Hand off with context** — if passing a guest to a practitioner, teacher, or specialist, carry the context so the guest never repeats themselves.
4. **Hospitality** — offer to seat, take coats, offer water/refreshment as appropriate. Rifq in every gesture.
5. **Close the loop** — no one leaves confused or unhelped; a warm farewell.

**FOH standards:**
- Presence over paperwork — the guest in front of you comes before the screen.
- The institution's voice: calm, precise, warm; never rushed, never salesy.
- **Never improvise clinical or scholarly answers** — route them to the qualified person (Blueprint Doc 01 §D). "Let me bring someone who can answer that properly" is always the right move.
- Discretion — clinic guests are received with privacy; nothing about who is here or why is discussed where others can hear.

## 3.6 The in-person guest experience

Mirrors the digital guest arc (guest → student/client → member) in physical form:
- **Browsing the Apothecary:** unpressured; staff available, never hovering. Product knowledge is honest and four-layer framed (Chapter 01, 05).
- **Arriving for a clinic:** private, calm, dignified reception (Chapter 06).
- **Arriving for a course:** welcomed as an expected student; oriented to the studio and facilities (Chapter 07).
- **Collecting an order / return:** frictionless; handled with the same care as a sale.
- **Every failure state designed:** a delayed practitioner, an out-of-stock item, a guest who arrives distressed — each has a gracious in-person recovery (mirrors Blueprint Doc 01).

## 3.7 Facilities, cleaning & maintenance

- **Cleaning schedule:** a documented schedule per zone — daily, deeper weekly, periodic deep-clean. Clinical and dispensary cleaning follows Chapters 04/06/05 (these are safety, not housekeeping).
- **Maintenance:** a reactive log (report faults immediately) and a planned schedule (servicing of equipment, alarms, extinguishers, ventilation, fridges). Nothing safety-critical runs overdue.
- **Environmental care:** we minimise waste, dispose responsibly (incl. clinical waste via licensed carrier, Chapter 04), and treat the space with the ṭahārah our values require.

## 3.8 Security & access

- **Key-holding** is a named responsibility; keys/access are logged, never shared informally.
- **Access levels** match zones and roles (least-privilege; mirrors digital access, Chapter 11).
- **Visitors and contractors** are signed in, escorted in staff-only zones, and never left alone with guest data or clinical areas.
- **Incidents** (theft, intrusion, threatening behaviour) follow the incident procedure (Chapter 04) and are logged.
- **Cash and valuables** secured; the dispensary's higher-value stock is access-controlled.

## 3.9 Acceptance criteria (Headquarters)

- [ ] Every zone has a defined reset state and access level.
- [ ] Opening and closing SOPs are signed daily; nothing opens until safety/cleanliness confirmed.
- [ ] Clinical and dispensary readiness are confirmed before use, to their hygiene standards.
- [ ] Clean-desk / physical-privacy rule enforced at close.
- [ ] FOH welcomes within seconds, routes clinical/scholarly questions, and protects clinic-guest discretion.
- [ ] Cleaning and maintenance schedules exist and never run safety-critical items overdue.
- [ ] Keys, access, visitors, and incidents are controlled and logged.

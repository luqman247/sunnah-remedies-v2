# Chapter 06 · The Clinic: Consultations, Hijama & Clinical Governance

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Clinical Lead · Classification: Internal (clinical) · Review: Annual, and on any incident or guidance change
> *Critical note:* This chapter is the **operational and governance wrapper** around clinical practice — how the clinic runs safely and ethically. It is **not** a clinical training manual and does not teach treatment technique. All clinical practice is performed only by appropriately trained, and where required licensed/registered, practitioners, following recognised professional standards and the institution's clinical-governance framework, which is set and approved by the Clinical Review Board and qualified clinical counsel. Where professional guidance differs from this text, professional guidance governs.

---

## 6.1 The clinical promise

We complement healthcare; we never replace it, and we never discourage anyone from seeking it. Every guest who enters the clinic is met with the same standard: **safe, consenting, within scope, honestly framed, and treated with dignity.** The Clinical Review Board holds veto over all clinical practice (Blueprint §4.2).

## 6.2 Scope of practice

- Practitioners work **only within their trained competence** and the institution's defined scope.
- We **do not diagnose medical conditions, promise cures, or replace medical treatment.**
- **Red-flag symptoms** and anything outside scope are **referred to appropriate medical care** — a referral is a success of the safety system, not a failure of service.
- Any claim made to a guest about a treatment matches the evidence and the four-layer honesty standard (Chapter 01); prophetic-medicine framing is tradition and guidance, never guaranteed cure.

## 6.3 Practitioner conduct

- Hold amānah with the guest's body, trust, and confidences.
- Maintain professional boundaries at all times.
- **Chaperoning and gender-appropriate care** offered as standard: same-gender practitioner and/or chaperone arrangements available and, where appropriate, default (Chapter 04 safeguarding).
- Never work while unwell in a way that risks the guest (IPC, Chapter 04).
- Keep clinical knowledge current through CPD.
- Practise the institution's rifq — unhurried, gentle, reassuring.

## 6.4 The consultation SOP

**Trigger:** a booked consultation (Blueprint Doc 01 §B5 governs the booking journey).

1. **Prepare the room** (§6.6) and review the guest's intake/history.
2. **Welcome** privately and with dignity; confirm identity and reason for visit.
3. **Confirm consent** for the consultation and explain what it is and isn't.
4. **Listen and assess** within scope; note relevant history, medications, and red flags.
5. **Screen for contraindications** to anything that might be advised or performed.
6. **Advise honestly** — within scope, evidence-matched, four-layer framed; provide any guidance in writing where helpful.
7. **Refer** to medical care where indicated — clearly and supportively.
8. **Record** the consultation in the clinical record (§6.9).
9. **Aftercare and follow-up** arranged where appropriate (Blueprint Doc 01 §B5).

## 6.5 Hijama (wet cupping) — operational SOP

**This SOP governs the safe operation of a Hijama session, not its technique.** Technique is a matter of accredited clinical training. Preconditions below are mandatory.

**Preconditions (all must be true before any session):**
- [ ] Practitioner appropriately trained and, where required, licensed/registered
- [ ] Local-authority special-treatment licence/registration held (Chapter 04.2)
- [ ] Room prepared to clinical-clean and IPC standard (§6.6, Chapter 04.6)
- [ ] Informed consent obtained and recorded (§6.7)
- [ ] Screening and contraindications checked; no red flags
- [ ] Single-use, sterile disposables ready; sharps container in place
- [ ] Emergency protocol and first-aider available

**During the session (operational duties):**
1. Confirm consent and comfort; maintain privacy and chaperoning arrangements.
2. Maintain strict **infection control** throughout — hand hygiene, PPE, clean/dirty separation, single-use items for anything contacting blood or broken skin.
3. Manage **sharps and blood safely** at every moment; never re-cap or reuse single-use items.
4. Monitor the guest's condition continuously; stop immediately and invoke the emergency protocol at any sign of an adverse reaction.
5. Keep the guest informed and reassured (rifq).

**After the session:**
1. **Aftercare guidance** given verbally and in writing (wound care, warning signs, when to seek medical help).
2. **Dispose** of all clinical waste and sharps into approved containers per Chapter 04.9; nothing single-use is retained.
3. **Decontaminate** the room and any reusable equipment to standard before the next guest (§6.6).
4. **Record** the session in the clinical record (§6.9).
5. **Follow-up** arranged where appropriate.

**Failure/exception:** any adverse event → emergency protocol (Chapter 04.5/4.7), record, review by Clinical Lead, and report per §6.10.

## 6.6 Room preparation & turnaround SOP

**Before every guest:**
- [ ] Surfaces cleaned/decontaminated to clinical standard
- [ ] Clean linens/coverings as required
- [ ] Single-use supplies stocked; sharps container present and not overfilled
- [ ] Waste containers correct and available
- [ ] Hand-hygiene facilities/sanitiser ready
- [ ] Room private, calm, dignified

**Between guests:** full decontamination and re-stock — **no guest ever meets a trace of another.** Clean/dirty separation absolute.

**End of day:** clinical close-down (Chapter 03.4) — deep clean, waste secured, records filed.

## 6.7 Informed consent SOP

- Consent is **obtained and recorded before** any consultation or treatment.
- The guest understands: what will happen, benefits and risks, alternatives, aftercare, and that this complements rather than replaces medical care.
- Consent is voluntary, informed, and may be withdrawn at any time.
- Special care for consent involving vulnerable adults (Chapter 04.11); minors treated only within a defined, safeguarded, lawful framework with appropriate consent.

## 6.8 Screening & contraindications SOP

- A screening step precedes treatment, checking history, medications, conditions, and contraindications appropriate to the treatment.
- Uncertain or red-flag cases are **deferred and referred**, not treated. The Clinical Review Board maintains the contraindication and red-flag reference; practitioners follow it.

## 6.9 Clinical records SOP

- Every consultation and treatment is recorded: history, consent, screening, what was done, advice/referrals given, aftercare, and any adverse event.
- Records are **confidential special-category health data** (Chapter 04.10) — stored securely, access-controlled, retained per legal requirement, never in filenames/URLs.
- Records are accurate, contemporaneous, and legible — an amānah to the guest and a protection for the practitioner.

## 6.10 Clinical governance & incident review

- **Clinical Review Board** owns clinical standards, the contraindication reference, audit, and sign-off on any new treatment or claim.
- **Clinical audit** on a schedule: consent completeness, IPC compliance, record quality, referral appropriateness, waste handling.
- **Adverse events and near-misses** are reported (Chapter 04.12), reviewed by the Clinical Lead/Board, and turned into improved controls.
- **Sharps-injury protocol** (staff): immediate first aid, occupational-health follow-up, recording, review.
- Complaints about clinical care are handled seriously, privately, and fed into governance.

## 6.11 Acceptance criteria (Clinic)

- [ ] All clinical practice is within scope, by trained (and where required licensed) practitioners, under Clinical Board governance.
- [ ] No diagnosis/cure claims; red flags referred to medical care.
- [ ] Informed consent recorded before every consultation/treatment.
- [ ] Screening and contraindication checks precede treatment; uncertain cases deferred and referred.
- [ ] IPC and single-use standards absolute; no cross-contamination between guests.
- [ ] Sharps and clinical waste handled and disposed safely via licensed carrier; sharps-injury protocol in place.
- [ ] Chaperoning and gender-appropriate care offered as standard.
- [ ] Confidential clinical records kept for every guest.
- [ ] Clinical audit and adverse-event review run on schedule.

# 00 — Institution Constitution

## Founding Declaration

Sunnah Remedies is an institute for the preservation, transmission, and responsible practice of *Tibb al-Nabawi* — Prophetic Medicine. It is not a brand. It is not a marketplace. It is an institution structured for long stewardship.

The institution exists to serve the next generation, not this quarter.

---

## Purpose

To provide:

1. **Scholarship** — primary-source research, graded references, published monographs
2. **Clinical care** — supervised Hijama practice, governed by clear standards
3. **Natural therapeutics** — remedies documented to origin, verified by laboratory analysis
4. **Education** — structured programmes with named faculty, published assessment, and institutional certification
5. **Pilgrimage** — educational travel ordered by purpose, reflection, and companionship

---

## Constitutional Principles

### 1. Knowledge Before Commerce

Every product, programme, or journey is documented before it is offered. The monograph precedes the transaction. Reading precedes purchase.

### 2. The Person Precedes Protocol

No remedy or treatment is dispensed without regard for the individual. Clinical governance exists to protect the patient, not the institution.

### 3. Healing is from Allah; the Remedy is a Means

The institution makes no claim to cure. It provides means, states limits plainly, and names what it does not know with the same care as what it does.

### 4. Beauty is an Obligation

Presentation, environment, documentation, and communication are held to the same standard as clinical practice. Aesthetic discipline is not ornamentation — it is institutional integrity made visible.

### 5. Institutional Transparency

Fees, limits, conflicts of interest, and governance are stated publicly. The Founding Charter is available for inspection. Nothing is hidden behind marketing.

### 6. The Waqf Model

The institution is structured for continuity — not exit. Revenue serves the mission. Growth is permitted only when it strengthens stewardship.

---

## Governance Structure

### Advisory Board

The Advisory Board provides scholarly, clinical, and institutional oversight. Members are listed by qualification and are accountable to the institution's principles.

### Faculty

All teaching staff are listed by:
- Name
- Qualification
- Chain of transmission (*isnad*)
- Clinical licence (where applicable)

### The Register

Graduates of the Academy who meet institutional standards are entered on the Register and held to continuing professional development.

## Decision Framework

When any decision must be made — editorial, clinical, commercial, or architectural — the institution asks:

1. Does this strengthen scholarship?
2. Does this protect the patient?
3. Does this preserve trust?
4. Does this favour longevity over convenience?
5. Would this be appropriate for a respected university, medical institution, or scholarly publishing house?

If any answer is no, the decision is not taken.

---

## Limits

The institution plainly states:

- It does not claim to cure any condition
- It does not replace conventional medical advice
- It does not dispense without documentation
- It does not teach without supervision
- It does not market without evidence
- It does not grow without governance

---

## Longevity Commitment

This platform is designed to serve the institution for a minimum of twenty years without fundamental redesign. Every architectural, editorial, and visual decision is tested against this standard.

The institution is not building for the current season. It is building for the generation that follows.

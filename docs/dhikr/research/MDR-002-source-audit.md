## 6. Arabic source wording

**Evidence label: directly inspected source-page content relayed by the content owner** (Tirmidhi 3388, Ibn Majah 3869 — substantively identical; corroborated by Riyad as-Salihin 1457). This is not identical in evidentiary weight to primary text independently fetched by this system.

```
مَا مِنْ عَبْدٍ يَقُولُ فِي صَبَاحِ كُلِّ يَوْمٍ وَمَسَاءِ كُلِّ لَيْلَةٍ:
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الَّذِي لاَ يَضُرُّ مَعَ اسْمِهِ شَيْءٌ فِي الأَرْضِ وَلاَ فِي السَّمَاءِ وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ،
ثَلاَثَ مَرَّاتٍ، إِلاَّ لَمْ يَضُرَّهُ شَيْءٌ
```

**Closing-clause note**: the relayed transcription of the closing clause read literally as "فَيَضُرُّهُ شَيْءٌ" ("then something will harm him") — the opposite of the hadith's well-established meaning; the negation appears to have been omitted or corrupted in relay. The wording above, "إِلاَّ لَمْ يَضُرَّهُ شَيْءٌ", is the standard formula supported by the surrounding narration context and by every English-translation source found in the earlier research pass, and has been **reconstructed** on that basis. It is **not** independently transcribed from the live page, and must be treated as reconstructed, not confirmed, until a human researcher independently re-confirms it character-for-character against the live Sunnah.com page.

**Finding**: the transcribed du'a portion of MDR-002 matches the relayed primary wording exactly at the word level. The only differences are (a) commas inserted in the source document for readability, and (b) alternate but equivalent diacritic/hamza notation conventions (لَا/لاَ, الْأَرْضِ/الأَرْضِ) — no letter, word, or meaning-level variation. `wordingMatchStatus` is set to **`minor-orthographic-variation`**, reflecting punctuation- and notation-only differences, not a substantive wording discrepancy. This is kept despite `sourceResearchStatus` remaining `scholarly-review-required` — the two fields answer different questions (wording comparison vs. overall research completeness).

**"All-Seeing" correction**: an earlier version of this audit, working from search-indexed English translations only, flagged that Ibn Majah's indexed English rendering of "السميع" as "All-Seeing" appeared inconsistent with the other collections' "the Hearing/Hearer" and left it as an open discrepancy. That flag is corrected here: **السميع** unambiguously means **the All-Hearing** in Arabic — this is settled vocabulary, not a live question. "All-Seeing" was an English translation anomaly on that specific indexed page (a mistranslation or transcription artefact of that rendering), not an Arabic wording variant, not a chain disagreement, and not a source-text conflict. The relayed Arabic in §6 above (وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ) is unaffected and consistent across every source in this audit.

## 8. Timing evidence

Found in directly inspected, user-relayed source-page content, part of the hadith's own opening frame in both Tirmidhi 3388 and Ibn Majah 3869: **"فِي صَبَاحِ كُلِّ يَوْمٍ وَمَسَاءِ كُلِّ لَيْلَةٍ"** ("in the morning of every day and the evening of every night"). This is the hadith's own wording, not derived from Tirmidhi's chapter heading (which happens to be consistent with it) or from any later compilation's placement. `morningSpecificStatus` is set to **`morning-and-evening`** on this basis — primary wording is substantially verified, though independent (agent-fetched) confirmation remains outstanding.

## 9. Repetition evidence

Found in directly inspected, user-relayed source-page content, part of the hadith's own wording in both Tirmidhi 3388 and Ibn Majah 3869: **"ثَلاَثَ مَرَّاتٍ"** ("three times"), positioned after the recited formula and before the virtue clause. The three repetitions apply to the whole supplication as a single unit, not to any sub-part of it. This directly and specifically supports the source document's own `3x` annotation — the document's annotation is not treated as evidence by itself; the narration's own wording is the evidence, and the two happen to agree.

## 10. Virtue / protection evidence

Found in directly inspected, user-relayed source-page content, with the closing clause reconstructed per §6: **"إِلاَّ لَمْ يَضُرَّهُ شَيْءٌ"** — "nothing will harm him." This is the narrower, directly-supported claim. No "until morning" or "until evening" durational limitation is stated in the relayed wording itself, and none is asserted here — the earlier draft of this audit's broader "until the next recitation" framing has been removed as unsupported by the primary text actually inspected.

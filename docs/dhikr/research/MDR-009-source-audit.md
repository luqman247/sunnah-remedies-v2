## 13. Attribution analysis

Reported from Anas ibn Malik, via two distinct chains per the directly-fetched islamweb.net fatwa page: (a) Baqiyyah ibn al-Walid <- Muslim ibn Ziyad <- Anas ibn Malik (Abu Dawud, al-Tirmidhi, al-Diya' al-Maqdisi); (b) Muhammad ibn Isma'il ibn Abi Fudayk <- 'Abd al-Rahman ibn 'Abd al-Majid <- Hisham ibn al-Ghaz ibn Rabi'ah <- Makhul al-Dimashqi <- Anas ibn Malik (Abu Dawud, al-Nasa'i, Ibn al-Sunni — this is the specific chain shown on the directly-fetched islamweb.net Sunan Abi Dawud hosting for hadith 5069 itself). This narrator identity is corroborated without exception by every source consulted. Both routes are reported as narrowly transmitted: khaledalsabt.com reports Baqiyyah ibn al-Walid as a known mudallis, with a critique (attributed by the fetched summary to a name rendered "al-Tahrani," not independently confirmed) that the hadith is not known from Anas except through Baqiyyah's own chain; whether Makhul al-Dimashqi is established to have heard directly from Anas ibn Malik (a connectivity/inqita' question specific to route (b)) was not addressed by any source consulted in this pass and remains unresolved. The Prophetic attribution and introductory frame ("man qala hina yusbihu aw yumsi...") is reported within the same tool-mediated quotations, not independently re-verified against a raw manuscript or print edition.

## 16. Repetition analysis

`sourceDocumentAnnotations` contains `"4x"`, pre-populated in Stage 3A as `repetitionCount: 4` — this was independently evaluated, not simply trusted. The source-document "4x" is corroborated by all five directly-fetched/tool-mediated sources (the primary Abu Dawud hosting, the fatwa page, khaledalsabt.com, hadithcommentary.com, and WebSearch synthesis) as matching the narration's own graduated reward structure specifically: reciting once yields a quarter emancipation from the Fire, twice yields half, three times yields three-quarters, and four times yields complete emancipation from the Fire. The source-document's "4x" therefore corresponds specifically to the narration's "complete emancipation" tier, not to an arbitrary editorial display choice. This finding is separate from, and not dependent on, the disputed isnad grade (§18): the repetition-and-reward structure's wording is consistently reported, but the chain carrying the whole narration is separately disputed, and the repetition structure was not separately graded by any source consulted.

## 17. Virtue/protection analysis

## 18. Grading/authenticity analysis

`hadithGrading` is populated: **"Disputed among named authorities: graded hasan/sound by al-Nawawi, Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Diya' al-Maqdisi, Ibn Hajar, and Ibn Baz; explicitly weakened by al-Albani, reported as identifying jahala (unidentified narrators) in the chain."** `gradingAuthority` reads: **"Multiple named classical and modern authorities, in explicit disagreement with one another; all positions reported via a tool-mediated secondary source (khaledalsabt.com), not independently verified against each scholar's own primary work in this pass."** Several separate conclusions are distinguished, not conflated:

1. The underlying narration is found in Sunan Abi Dawud, hadith 5069, and corroborated by three further modern discussion sources (§8–§10, §13).
2. Five named authorities (al-Nawawi, Ibn al-Qayyim, al-Diya' al-Maqdisi, Ibn Hajar, Ibn Baz) graded it hasan/sound.
3. One named authority (al-Albani) explicitly weakened it, citing jahala in the chain — this is a genuine authenticity disagreement, not a route or edition-numbering discrepancy.
4. The dispute was not traced to a specific one of the two reported isnads in this pass (§13) — it is not established whether al-Albani's weakening addresses one route, both routes, or the hadith as generally transmitted.
5. The reward ladder (§16–§17) belongs to this same disputedly-graded narration — it is not more secure than the underlying isnad dispute.
6. No source consulted in this pass was independently checked against al-Albani's own primary works (Da'if Sunan Abi Dawud, Da'if al-Jami' al-Saghir, Silsilat al-Ahadith al-Da'ifa).

It does **not** mean the hadith is settled as sahih merely because a majority of named scholars graded it hasan, and it does **not** mean al-Albani's weakening is dispositive merely because it is more recent.

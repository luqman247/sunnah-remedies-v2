## 4. Segmentation decision

Segmentation was judged necessary on structural grounds alone: the text contains multiple grammatical/thematic boundaries (new vocatives, a shift from declaration to petition, changes of theme). Six clauses (`MDR-004-A` through `MDR-004-F`) were identified on the following grounds. **A grammatical boundary is distinguished throughout from a source-attribution claim, which is in turn distinguished from proof of separate origins** — none of the boundaries below is treated as verified evidence of separate narrations by itself.

- **A→B**: end of the Talbiyah-style repeated-response formula; new vocative "اللَّهُمَّ" opens an oath/vow theme.
- **B→C**: new vocative "اللَّهُمَّ" opens a parallel-structure theme (prayers/curses) mirroring clause B's grammar.
- **C→D**: explicit first-person petition marker "إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ" ("I ask You") opens a new theme; a reported source lead (not directly confirmed) separately associates this wording with a different narration (Ammar ibn Yasir) than the reported Zayd ibn Thabit lead behind A–C.
- **D→E**: new vocative "اللَّهُمَّ" opens a Qur'anic-style address distinct in theme and, per a reported (not directly confirmed) source lead, distinct from clause D's lead.
- **E→F**: grammatically the least clean boundary in the record — clause F continues the same "وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ..." testimony chain begun in clause E. This boundary is a **provisional source-analysis boundary, not a verified hadith boundary**: it is drawn primarily because the shortest located quotation associated with clause E's reported source lead ends before clause F's content begins, and no source has been confirmed for clause F specifically. `boundaryConfidence` for this clause is recorded as `medium`, not `high`, to reflect this.

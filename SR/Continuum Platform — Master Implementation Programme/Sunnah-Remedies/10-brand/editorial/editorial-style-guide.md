# 04 — Editorial Style Guide

## Editorial Philosophy

The institution publishes with the care of a university press. Every word is weighed. Every claim carries a source. Every limit is stated before any promise.

The editorial voice belongs to the institution — not to a writer, not to a marketer, not to a personality.

---

## Voice Characteristics

### Declarative

The institution states. It does not persuade, convince, or cajole.

> "The Academy trains practitioners through structured clinical programmes assessed by independent examination."

Not: "Join our amazing Academy and transform your career!"

### Restrained

Enthusiasm is replaced by precision. Excitement is replaced by care.

> "Each batch is laboratory-verified and documented in a monograph."

Not: "We're so excited to bring you the finest quality honey!"

### Accountable

Every statement implies a source, a standard, or a limit.

> "Faculty are listed by qualification and *isnad*."

Not: "Our expert team of instructors..."

### Dignified

The reader is addressed as a thoughtful person capable of careful reading.

> "Reading precedes dispensation."

Not: "Check out our products before you buy!"

---

## Language Rules

### Terminology

| Use | Do Not Use |
|---|---|
| Remedy | Product |
| Dispensary | Shop / Store |
| Monograph | Product description |
| Programme | Course (unless referring to short courses) |
| Faculty | Instructor / Teacher |
| Practitioner | Therapist |
| Patient | Client / Customer |
| The institution | We / Our company |
| Acquisition | Purchase / Buy |
| Stated limits | Disclaimer |
| Certificate of analysis | Lab report |
| Prophetic Medicine | Islamic medicine |
| *Tibb al-Nabawi* | Alternative medicine |

### Prohibited Words & Phrases

- Revolutionary
- Exclusive
- Limited edition
- Best-selling
- Amazing / Incredible
- Transform / Transformation
- Unlock / Discover (as marketing verbs)
- Journey (as metaphor — permitted only for Sacred Journeys)
- Wellness
- Holistic (unless in specific clinical context)
- Natural (as a selling point — state what it *is*)
- Premium (describe the material quality instead)
- Any urgency language (hurry, last chance, don't miss)

### Formatting Conventions

- Dates: 1 January 2025 (no ordinals, no abbreviations)
- Times: 09:00, 14:30 (24-hour)
- Currency: £2,400 (comma separator, no decimals unless pence)
- Arabic terms: italicised on first use, with translation in parentheses
- Qur'anic references: Surah name, ayah number (e.g. Al-Nahl: 69)
- Hadith references: Collection, narrator, hadith number, grade
- Measurements: metric primary, with common equivalents where helpful

## Content Types

### Monograph

A scholarly record of a remedy. Structure:

1. Name (English, Arabic, botanical)
2. Historical context
3. Prophetic references (graded)
4. Traditional scholarship
5. Traditional usage
6. Scientific evidence (established and emerging)
7. Provenance
8. Laboratory verification
9. Contraindications
10. Suggested use
11. Storage
12. FAQ

Tone: clinical, precise, accountable.

### Article

A published piece for the Knowledge Library. Structure:

1. Title
2. Author and date
3. Abstract / excerpt
4. Body (with rich formatting)
5. References
6. Related reading

Tone: scholarly, accessible, cited.

### Programme Description

A published prospectus for a course or diploma. Structure:

1. Programme name and tier
2. What it is (3-5 sentences)
3. For whom
4. What it asks of the student
5. Curriculum
6. Faculty
7. Learning outcomes
8. Assessment
9. Certification
10. Fee and structure
11. Entry requirements
12. FAQ

Tone: precise, honest, complete.

### Journey Description

A published programme for a sacred journey. Structure:

1. Journey name
2. Meaning and purpose
3. Dates and logistics
4. For whom
5. What it asks
6. Itinerary
7. Scholars
8. Preparation
9. Accommodation
10. FAQ

Tone: reflective, honest, inviting without urging.

## Citation Standards

### Qur'anic References

Format: Arabic text, translation, reference.

> *"And your Lord inspired the bee: 'Take for yourself among the mountains, houses, and among the trees and in that which they construct.'"*
> — Al-Nahl: 68 (Sahih International)

### Hadith References

Format: Translation, source, narrator, grade.

> "Make use of the two cures: honey and the Qur'an."
> — Sunan Ibn Majah · Hadith 3452 · Narrated by Ibn Mas'ud · Graded Hasan by al-Albani

### Academic Sources

Format: Author (Year). Title. Publication. DOI/URL.

> Al-Jawziyyah, I.Q. (d. 1350). *Zad al-Ma'ad fi Hady Khayr al-'Ibad*. Vol. 4.

## Editorial Workflow

### States

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | Being written or revised |
| In Review | Under editorial or scholarly review |
| Published | Live on the public website |
| Scheduled | Approved, awaiting publication date |
| Archived | Removed from public view, retained in system |

### Review Process

1. Author creates draft
2. Draft submitted for review
3. Reviewer checks: accuracy, sources, tone, formatting
4. Revisions if necessary
5. Approval with date stamp
6. Publication

### Version Control

Every published document carries:
- Version number
- Last updated date
- Revision notes (internal)
- Author attribution

---

## Heading Hierarchy

Pages follow a strict heading hierarchy:

- `h1` — Page title (one per page, often in the hero)
- `h2` — Section headings
- `h3` — Subsection headings
- `h4` — Detail headings (rare)

Never skip heading levels. Never use headings for visual sizing.

---

## Lists

- Ordered lists for sequential steps or ranked items
- Unordered lists for non-sequential collections
- No more than 7 items without subgrouping
- Each item is a complete phrase or sentence (consistent within the list)

---

## Abbreviations

- Spell out on first use: Continuing Professional Development (CPD)
- Common Islamic terms do not need expansion after first use
- Academic abbreviations follow standard convention

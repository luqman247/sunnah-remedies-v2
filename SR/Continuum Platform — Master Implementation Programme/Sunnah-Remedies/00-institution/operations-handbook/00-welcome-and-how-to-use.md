## Assalāmu ʿalaykum, and welcome

You are holding the operating manual of Sunnah Remedies — the same standard whether you are a practitioner in the clinic, a photographer in the studio, a developer at your desk, an editor at the reading room, a teacher in the Academy, or the person who unlocks the front door at dawn.

This is not a rulebook to be feared. It is the accumulated care of an institution that has decided to do things properly. Everything in it exists so that a guest who walks through our doors — or opens one of our emails — meets one consistent, honest, excellent institution, no matter who happens to be on shift.

**On your first day, read Chapters 00, 01, and 02, and the chapter for your role. That is enough to begin. The rest is reference for when you need it.**

---

## What this handbook is (and what it is not)

**It is** the internal operating manual for our first headquarters and the people in it: every process, checklist, quality standard, SOP, approval, workflow, and responsibility that runs the institution day to day.

**It is not** the digital/strategic blueprint. That already exists — the *Operational Blueprint* and its ten manuals from the previous phase (referred to throughout as **the Blueprint** and by document number, e.g. *Blueprint Doc 02*). Where the Blueprint says *what the institution stands for and how its digital operations run*, this Handbook says *how the people and the building actually do it*. When the two meet — a review chain, a naming convention, a duty of care — this Handbook points you to the Blueprint rather than repeating it.

**The order of authority** (memorise this):
1. The law and our regulatory obligations.
2. Safety and safeguarding.
3. The Operational Blueprint (the institution's constitution).
4. This Handbook.
5. Local team practice.

If any two conflict, the higher number yields to the lower. When in doubt, stop and escalate (Chapter 12).

## The one idea beneath everything

Every decision at Sunnah Remedies sits on two ledgers at once:

- **The Commercial Ledger** — does it serve, delight, and sustain the institution?
- **The Integrity Ledger** — is it scholarly sound, clinically safe, and honestly framed?

**The Integrity Ledger holds veto.** No amount of commercial success overrules a scholarly or clinical objection. This single asymmetry is what makes us an institution and not a shop. It governs how you write a label, greet a guest, grade a hadith, prepare a clinic room, and answer an email. If you internalise nothing else, internalise this.

## How to read an SOP in this Handbook

Every Standard Operating Procedure follows one shape, so you always know where to look:

- **Purpose** — why this exists and what "good" looks like.
- **Owner** — the role accountable for it.
- **Trigger** — what starts it.
- **Steps** — numbered, in order; do them in order.
- **Standard** — the quality bar it must meet.
- **Approvals** — who must sign off, and when.
- **Records** — what must be logged and where.
- **Failure/exception** — what to do when it goes wrong.

Checklists are marked with `[ ]` and are meant to be worked through, not skimmed.

## A few house conventions

- **"Guest," not "user."** People who visit us — in person or online — are guests first, then students/clients, then members. Our language reflects that.
- **The slow lane.** Anyone may pause any decision that touches scholarship or safety, without penalty, until it is verified. Using it is a sign of good judgement, never of weakness.
- **Escalate early.** It is always better to raise a hand than to guess on something that touches a guest's health, faith, or trust.
- **Document as you go.** Write things so the person who inherits your role in ten years can do it without meeting you.

# Chapter 07 · The Academy: Teaching Operations

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Dean of the Academy · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual
> *Companion:* Blueprint Doc 06 governs the course lifecycle (applications, screening, certification, alumni). This chapter governs **teaching at HQ** — the studio, the faculty, the day.

---

## 7.1 The teaching standard

We teach as transformation, not information transfer (Harvard discipline). A graduate must be able to **practise and teach from** what they learned, without correction (Blueprint §9). Everything in the teaching studio serves that outcome, and where teaching involves clinical skills (e.g. Hijama), **safety governs above all** (Chapters 04, 06).

## 7.2 Faculty responsibilities

- Teach to the **Board-approved course dossier** and its learning outcomes (Blueprint Doc 06) — never off-syllabus into unverified content.
- Uphold the **four layers of honesty** in everything taught (Chapter 01): what tradition establishes vs. interpretation vs. our practice.
- Hold **scholarly and clinical accuracy** — anything touching the tradition or the body is already Board-reviewed; faculty transmit it faithfully and flag anything they believe needs re-review (the slow lane applies to teaching too).
- Assess **fairly and rigorously** to the defined competence standard.
- Model the institution's adab — punctuality, preparation, rifq with students.
- Maintain their own CPD and subject currency.

## 7.3 Teaching studio preparation SOP

**Before a session:**
- [ ] Studio set to the session plan (layout, materials, AV)
- [ ] Handouts/materials are the current approved versions (version control, Blueprint Doc 02)
- [ ] Attendance/registration ready
- [ ] For **practical clinical teaching**: the space meets clinical-clean and IPC standards (Chapter 04.6, 06.6), single-use supplies and sharps/clinical-waste containers in place, and safety briefing prepared
- [ ] Accessibility and comfort checked (seating, temperature, prayer/break timings)

**Turnaround/close:** studio reset; any clinical materials handled per Chapters 04/06; nothing single-use retained; space returned to reset state (Chapter 03).

## 7.4 Course delivery SOP

1. **Welcome and orient** students to the studio, facilities, prayer space, breaks, and safety (fire exits, first aid).
2. **Set expectations** — outcomes, assessment, code of conduct.
3. **Teach** to the dossier, checking understanding as you go.
4. **Practical components** (where applicable) run under strict safety supervision (§7.5).
5. **Assess** against the competence standard (§7.6).
6. **Close** each session with consolidation and next steps.
7. **Record** attendance, progress, and any issues.

## 7.5 Practical & clinical teaching safety

Where a course teaches a clinical skill:

- Practical work happens **only** in an appropriately prepared, IPC-compliant space (Chapters 04, 06).
- Faculty supervise directly; students never perform on another person outside the supervised, consented, safe framework.
- **Consent** is obtained from anyone acting as a practice subject; screening applies.
- **Infection control, single-use, sharps and clinical-waste** rules are identical to the clinic (Chapters 04.6, 04.9, 06) — teaching is not an exception to safety.
- The course makes the **licensing reality** explicit: competence taught here does not itself authorise practice; students must license/register locally as required before practising (Blueprint Doc 06).

## 7.6 Assessment integrity SOP

- Assessment is **competence-based** against a transparent standard — knowledge *and*, for clinical skills, demonstrated safe practical competence.
- Assessment is fair, consistent, and documented; the same standard applies to everyone.
- **Failure is handled privately and supportively**, with a defined re-assessment path (Blueprint Doc 06) — never a public fail.
- **Academic honesty:** plagiarism or misrepresentation is addressed; the certificate must mean what it says.

## 7.7 Certification SOP (issuance)

- A certificate is **issued only on demonstrated competence** — attendance alone never earns it.
- The certificate is an **artefact** (designed, dignified) and carries a unique reference and a verifiable issued-record (Blueprint Doc 06; DAM restricted store, Doc 04).
- It states honestly **what it certifies and its scope limits**, and the student's responsibility to license locally for clinical practice.
- **CPD hours** recorded; graduate enters the alumni fellowship and, where eligible, the practitioner registry.

## 7.8 Student care & conduct

- Students are guests becoming members — met with warmth and respect.
- A **code of conduct** applies (respect, safety, academic honesty, confidentiality of any practice subjects).
- **Pastoral support:** students struggling academically or personally are supported, within appropriate boundaries and safeguarding (Chapter 04.11).
- Feedback is collected every cohort and feeds continuous improvement (Blueprint Doc 06).

## 7.9 Records

- Enrolment, attendance, assessment outcomes, certificates issued, CPD, and feedback are recorded and retained.
- Student PII handled per data protection (Chapter 04.10, Chapter 11); issued certificates in the restricted store.

## 7.10 Acceptance criteria (Academy)

- [ ] Faculty teach only to Board-approved dossiers, four-layer honest, faithfully transmitting reviewed content.
- [ ] Studio prepared to session plan; practical clinical teaching meets full IPC/safety standards.
- [ ] Practical work is supervised, consented, and safe; safety is never waived for teaching.
- [ ] Assessment is competence-based, fair, documented; failure handled privately with a re-assessment path.
- [ ] Certificates issued only on competence, are verifiable artefacts, and state scope + licensing reality.
- [ ] Student care, conduct, safeguarding, feedback, and records are all maintained.

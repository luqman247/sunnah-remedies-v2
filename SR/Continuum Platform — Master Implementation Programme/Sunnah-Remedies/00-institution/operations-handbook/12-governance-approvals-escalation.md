# Chapter 12 · Governance, Approvals & Escalation

> *Document control* — Version 1.0 · Custodian: Standards Council · Classification: Internal · Review: Annual
> *Companion:* Blueprint §5–§7 sets the governance model. This chapter is the **staff-facing "how do I get a decision made" guide.**

---

## 12.1 Who decides what

**The Standards Council** — the supreme operational body. Chaired by the CXO, with the Editor-in-Chief, Clinical Lead, a Scholarly Board representative, Head of Media, and Head of Systems. Resolves conflicts, owns the standards and this Handbook, adjudicates the two-ledger tension.

**The Scholarly Review Board** — verifies everything touching the Islamic tradition (hadith grading/attribution, Qur'an references, the four layers, prophetic-medicine framing). Veto power.

**The Clinical Review Board** — verifies everything touching the body (claims, contraindications, safety, scope). Veto power.

**Division leads** — decide within their standards (Chapter 02 org).

**The Brand Custodian** — guards the visual identity, editorial system, and voice.

## 12.2 The three tiers of decision (how to know who decides)

- **Tier 1 — Routine.** Inside an existing standard. **You decide** and log it. (e.g. restock, schedule a shoot, publish a pre-approved content type.)
- **Tier 2 — Judgement.** A standard exists but the case is at its edge. **Your accountable lead decides,** consulting the relevant Board. (e.g. a borderline health claim, an unusual journey applicant.)
- **Tier 3 — Precedent.** No standard exists, or the decision changes the institution. **The Standards Council decides** and writes the new standard. (e.g. a new product category, a new treatment, a new AI capability, an international expansion.)

**The golden rule (Blueprint §5):** anything touching scholarship or clinical safety is *never* decided by one person alone — it is accountable to a Board or the Council.

## 12.3 The approval matrix (quick reference)

| To do this… | You need sign-off from… |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Publish an article | Scholarly + Clinical (if health) + Editorial |
| Launch a product | Clinical + Scholarly + Editorial + Head of Apothecary (via launch checklist) |
| Make a health claim | Clinical Board (+ Scholarly if tradition, + legal for regulated claims) |
| Grade/cite a hadith | Scholarly Board |
| Offer a new treatment | Clinical Board + Standards Council (Tier 3) + licensing confirmed |
| Run a new course | Scholarly + Clinical Boards + Dean |
| Accept a journey applicant | Clinical reviewer (health screening) + Director of Journeys |
| Publish an image | Head of Media QC (+ product accuracy, releases, scholarly if sensitive) |
| Deploy a higher-risk system change | Head of Systems (+ relevant Board if it touches content) |
| Introduce AI into a workflow | Standards Council + relevant Board (Tier 3) |
| Change a customer-facing policy/tone | CXO (+ Standards Council) |
| Delete data / change access / move funds | Authorised human only — never automated; per policy |

## 12.4 The slow lane (your right and duty)

Any colleague — at any level — may pull any decision that touches scholarship or safety into the **slow lane**: a deliberate hold that removes time pressure until the matter is verified. Nothing ships from the slow lane until the objection is resolved.

**Using the slow lane is a sign of good judgement, never of obstruction.** It is the operational form of the institution's promise that integrity outranks speed. No one is ever penalised for using it in good faith.

## 12.5 How to escalate

1. **Start with your lead** for anything beyond your standard.
2. **Escalate to the relevant Board** for scholarly or clinical questions — do not guess.
3. **Escalate to the Standards Council** for conflicts between standards, precedent-setting decisions, or when your lead is unavailable and the matter can't wait.
4. **For grievances or concerns about conduct/ethics,** use the People route (Chapter 02.7); **safety/safeguarding concerns** go immediately to the safety/safeguarding lead (Chapter 04).

**When in doubt, raise a hand.** It is always better to ask than to guess on something that touches a guest's health, faith, money, or data.

## 12.6 Risk management (staff duty)

- The Standards Council owns the **risk register** (Blueprint §7), reviewed quarterly.
- **Everyone contributes:** report new risks and near-misses to your lead; they feed the register.
- Each risk has an owner, controls, and a review date. Safety and integrity risks are prioritised regardless of commercial cost.

## 12.7 Brand governance

- The **Brand Custodian** protects the identity, editorial system, and voice completed in earlier phases.
- Any use of brand assets follows brand review; the design system lives in the Media Library (Blueprint Doc 04).
- **One institution, one voice** across every artefact — a label, an email, a certificate, a room — must feel authored by the same hand (Blueprint Principle 5).

## 12.8 Decision log

- Tier 2 and Tier 3 decisions are **logged** — what was decided, by whom, why, and which standard it set or applied. This builds institutional memory and lets a successor understand *why* things are as they are.

## 12.9 Acceptance criteria (Governance)

- [ ] Everyone knows the three tiers and the golden rule (scholarship/safety never decided alone).
- [ ] The approval matrix is followed; nothing ships without required sign-offs.
- [ ] The slow lane is available to all, respected, and never penalised.
- [ ] Escalation routes are known and used; "raise a hand when in doubt" is the culture.
- [ ] Risks and near-misses are reported and fed to the register.
- [ ] Brand governance protects one-voice consistency.
- [ ] Tier 2/3 decisions are logged.

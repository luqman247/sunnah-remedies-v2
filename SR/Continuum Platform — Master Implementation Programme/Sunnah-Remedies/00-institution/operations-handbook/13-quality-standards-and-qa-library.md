## 13.1 What "quality" means here

Quality at Sunnah Remedies is not a department; it is a habit held by everyone. Its one-line test, per pillar, is pinned above every desk:

- **Apothecary:** *Would we read this product page aloud to the scholar who taught us and the doctor who treats our mother?*
- **Academy:** *Would a graduate teach from what we taught them, without correction?*
- **Sacred Journeys:** *Did the guest return changed, and safe?*
- **Knowledge Library:** *Could this article carry a museum label without embarrassment?*
- **Clinic:** *Did we stay honestly within what we know and what is safe?*
- **Front of House:** *Did the guest feel this was a serious, honest, gentle place?*

If any answer is "no," the Integrity Ledger has spoken — stop and fix it.

## 13.2 The seven institutional standards (summary)

Full standards live across the chapters; here they are in one place.

1. **Editorial** — nothing publishes without provenance and its review chain; four layers always distinguished; restraint enforced; every piece has a next-review date. *(Ch 09, Blueprint Doc 02.)*
2. **Clinical** — no claim exceeds evidence; consent, screening, IPC, scope; red flags referred; Clinical Board veto. *(Ch 04, 06.)*
3. **Scholarly** — every hadith attributed and graded; every reference verified; unverifiable attributions removed or flagged; prophetic medicine framed as guidance, never guaranteed cure; Scholarly Board veto. *(Ch 09, Blueprint Doc 02.)*
4. **Customer care** — acknowledge always; resolve or warm-handoff; never improvise clinical/scholarly answers; every failure state handled with care. *(Ch 03, Blueprint Doc 01.)*
5. **Writing** — one voice; honesty over persuasion; no superlatives or urgency; correct register; safety lines at the point of decision. *(Ch 09.)*
6. **Photography** — truthful, restrained, house-graded; RAW archived; metadata and rights complete; QC before publish. *(Ch 10, Blueprint Doc 03/04.)*
7. **Brand governance** — one institution, one voice across every artefact; brand review on all asset use. *(Ch 12.)*

## 13.3 Audit calendar

Quality is verified on a rhythm (Blueprint §8):

- **Daily** — opening/closing checklists; incident review; order/clinic/dispensary logs.
- **Weekly** — editorial planning; media pipeline (`_Incoming` cleared); cohort/journey check-ins.
- **Monthly** — Standards Council; analytics dashboards (integrity first); automation health check.
- **Quarterly** — light review of every chapter; risk-register review; content-audit sample; dispensary/clinic audit; access review.
- **Annually** — full review of the Handbook and Blueprint; full content and media audit; brand review; fire drill and safety review; roadmap re-plan.

## 13.5 Continuous improvement

- Every audit finding, complaint pattern, incident, and cohort/guest feedback feeds a **continuous-improvement log** owned by the relevant lead.
- Recurring themes escalate to the Standards Council and become **standard changes** — versioned into this Handbook (Chapter 14 document control).
- **A repeated question or error is a documentation gap** — we fix the source, not just the instance.

## 13.6 Acceptance criteria (Quality)

- [ ] The per-pillar quality test is known and used to settle real decisions.
- [ ] The seven standards are enforced across all chapters.
- [ ] The audit calendar runs on rhythm; findings are actioned, not filed.
- [ ] Every operational task has a checklist, indexed in the QA Library.
- [ ] Continuous-improvement log maintained; recurring issues become versioned standard changes.

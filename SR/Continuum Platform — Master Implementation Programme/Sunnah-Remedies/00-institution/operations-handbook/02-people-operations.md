At small scale, one person may hold several roles — but **the roles stay distinct even when the people do not**, so the institution can grow without reinventing itself.

## 2.2 Recruitment SOP

**Purpose:** hire for character first, craft second. Skills can be taught; amānah cannot.
**Owner:** Head of People with the hiring lead.

**Steps:**
1. Define the role against this Handbook (which chapter, which SOPs, which standards).
2. Write an honest advert — no inflation (we practise our own value).
3. Shortlist against role and values.
4. Interview in two parts: **craft** (can they do the work) and **character** (do they hold amānah, ṣidq, iḥsān). For clinical and teaching roles, verify qualifications and, where required, licences/registrations.
5. **Safeguarding-relevant roles** (clinic, teaching, journeys, anyone with access to vulnerable guests or children): appropriate background checks per law before contact with guests (Chapter 04).
6. Reference and right-to-work checks.
7. Offer, contract, and pre-start pack.

**Standard:** every hire can point to why they meet both the craft bar and the character bar.
**Records:** interview notes, checks, and decision rationale retained per data-protection policy (Chapter 04, 11).

## 2.3 Onboarding SOP

**Purpose:** a new colleague should feel expected, equipped, and oriented — never lost.
**Owner:** Head of People + the person's lead ("onboarding buddy" assigned).

**Before day one**
- [ ] Workspace, accounts, and access provisioned (least-privilege; Chapter 11)
- [ ] Handbook and Blueprint access granted
- [ ] Rota, prayer times, and first-week schedule shared
- [ ] Buddy assigned

**Day one**
- [ ] Welcome; tour of HQ and its zones (Chapter 03)
- [ ] Fire, first aid, and emergency briefing (Chapter 04) — *mandatory before working unsupervised*
- [ ] Read Chapters 00, 01, 02, and their role chapter
- [ ] Meet their pillar/function team

**Week one**
- [ ] Role-specific SOP walkthrough with lead
- [ ] Shadowing before solo work
- [ ] Systems, tools, and data-protection basics (Chapter 11)
- [ ] Confirm understanding of the two-ledger principle and the four layers of honesty

**First 30 / 60 / 90 days**
- [ ] 30: settled, doing core tasks supervised; first check-in
- [ ] 60: working independently on standard tasks; feedback both ways
- [ ] 90: probation review; competence signed against role standards

**Standard:** no colleague works unsupervised on any safety-, clinical-, or scholarship-critical task until signed competent by their lead.

## 2.4 Roles & responsibilities — the RACI habit

For any recurring task, be clear who is **R**esponsible (does it), **A**ccountable (owns the outcome), **C**onsulted, **I**nformed. The master roles matrix lives in Chapter 14. The governing rule (Blueprint §5): **any task touching scholarship or clinical safety is accountable to a Board or the Standards Council — never to one person acting alone.**

## 2.5 Performance & development

- **1:1s:** every colleague has a regular 1:1 with their lead — support, feedback, and blockers, both directions.
- **Development:** each person has a growth path. We fund learning that raises the standard (clinical CPD, scholarly study, craft skills).
- **Feedback culture:** honest, kind, timely, specific (Blueprint's responding-to-mistakes ethic — accountability without abasement). Praise in the open, correct in private.
- **Reviews:** a structured review at least annually against role standards and values.

## 2.6 Conduct, wellbeing & discipline

**Expected conduct:** the adab of Chapter 01, plus honesty, reliability, confidentiality, and respect. **Zero tolerance** for harassment, discrimination, dishonesty in records, breaches of guest confidentiality, and anything that endangers a guest's safety.

**Wellbeing:** workloads are humane; the institution does not glorify overwork (it contradicts iḥsān done sustainably). Support is available for stress, and colleagues are encouraged to raise strain early. We never foster a culture where people hide that they are struggling.

**Discipline (proportionate, lawful, documented):**
1. Informal conversation for minor, first-time issues.
2. Formal stages (warning → final warning → dismissal) for serious or repeated issues, following a fair, lawful process with the right to be accompanied and to respond.
3. **Gross misconduct** (e.g. endangering a guest, serious dishonesty, safeguarding breach) may warrant immediate suspension pending investigation.

All disciplinary matters are handled privately, fairly, and by the book, with HR/legal counsel.

## 2.7 Grievance

Any colleague may raise a concern — about treatment, safety, ethics, or a decision they believe wrong — without fear. Route: to your lead; if the concern involves your lead, to Head of People or the CXO; if it involves the leadership, to the Standards Council. **Whistleblowing** on safety, safeguarding, or integrity is protected and encouraged; retaliation is itself gross misconduct.

## 2.8 Leave & absence

- **Annual leave** per contract, requested in advance, planned so cover holds (clinic/dispensary/FOH never uncovered).
- **Sickness:** report early per the absence procedure; return-to-work conversations for extended absence. Anyone unwell who works with food (dispensary), guests (clinic/FOH), or in the studio must not attend if their condition risks others (Chapter 04, infection control).
- **Religious, family, and compassionate leave:** accommodated with rifq.

## 2.9 Offboarding SOP

**Purpose:** people leave well; the institution keeps its knowledge and its security.
**Steps:**
1. Handover document written (their processes, contacts, in-flight work) — the successor must be able to continue.
2. **Access revoked promptly** across all systems (Chapter 11) — a security-critical step, done the same day.
3. Institutional property, keys, and assets returned.
4. Records and PII they held are transferred to the right owner; nothing walks out.
5. Exit conversation — honest, gracious. We learn from why people leave.
6. Alumni goodwill: people should leave as friends of the institution.

## 2.10 Acceptance criteria (People Ops)

- [ ] Every role is defined against Handbook chapters and standards.
- [ ] No one works unsupervised on safety/clinical/scholarship tasks until signed competent.
- [ ] Safeguarding checks completed before guest contact for relevant roles.
- [ ] Onboarding, 1:1s, and reviews run for everyone; development paths exist.
- [ ] Discipline and grievance are fair, lawful, private, documented.
- [ ] Offboarding revokes access same-day and captures a handover.

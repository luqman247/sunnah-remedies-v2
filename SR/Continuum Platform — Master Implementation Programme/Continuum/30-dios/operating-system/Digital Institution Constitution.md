# Digital Institution Constitution

**The single highest authority for every digital product Sunnah Remedies ever builds.**

Status: Ratifiable draft · Version 1.0.0 · Same authority as `00 Institution Constitution`,
written in plain language for a reader who wants the whole picture in one pass.

---

## Why this exists

Sunnah Remedies is building a digital estate — an apothecary, an academy, a clinic, an
editorial and research arm — that must feel like **one institution**, hold to **one
standard**, and survive for **decades** while the tools underneath it change completely. AI
agents now do much of the work of building it. That makes the scarce thing not the
production of code, content or design, but the **trustworthy governance of change**: knowing
what is true, who changed it, on what authority, and whether it is verifiably correct.

This constitution makes that governance the institution's foundation. It is distilled from
everything the institution has specified — the Continuum platform architecture and its
ratified constitution, and the Sunnah Remedies Engineering Playbook and Operating System —
and states the timeless principles behind them once, at the highest authority. Everything
else in this repository derives from it.

## The one idea

> The institution's product is a **governed, verifiable model of truth.** Every website,
> application, document, product page and interface is a *projection* of that truth — a
> view, never the source. Humans and AI agents work as co‑equals: both may **propose**
> anything; neither is **trusted** about their own work. Change becomes true only through
> verification the proposer cannot influence, recorded immutably, under authority that is
> always granted and never assumed — with a human always standing at the gate of anything
> that cannot be undone.

Hold that idea and the rest follows.

## The non‑negotiables

These can never be violated by any product, person or tool. Weakening one requires a formal
constitutional amendment (`00`, Part 12).

1. **Truth is derived, never asserted.** Authority is never held by claiming it; it is traced
   to a ratified source.
2. **Verification precedes trust.** A change is trusted because it was verified, not because
   of who or what made it. Humans and agents meet the identical standard.
3. **The model is the source of truth; text is a view.** Where a page and the model disagree,
   the model wins and the page is the defect.
4. **No ambient authority.** Nothing can act by default. Every capability is granted, scoped
   and revocable. Absence of a grant is denial.
5. **A human gate on the irreversible.** Machine evidence is necessary for everything and
   sufficient for reversible things; publishing, deleting, migrating and deploying to
   production always also need a recorded human approval. An AI may never give it.
6. **Content is content.** Products, prices, courses, ingredients, articles and team live in
   the content model, editable by non‑engineers — never hardcoded.
7. **Reversibility over cleverness.** Prefer the design you can observe, bound and undo.
8. **Nothing is silently deleted.** Retired things become tombstones — recorded, explained,
   superseded — never vacancies.
9. **Everything is traceable.** Every change records what, who, why, and on what evidence.
10. **One institution, one standard.** Every product inherits this operating system. It may
    add stricter rules; it may never weaken these.

## How every future *website* is built

It composes the institution's design language (`01`) from shared tokens and primitives, so it
recognisably belongs to the institution. It renders content from the content model (`05`),
never hardcodes it. It passes the accessibility (`07`), performance (`08`) and discoverability
(`09`) standards as conditions of validity, not afterthoughts. It ships only through the
Review and Release gates (`14`, `15`).

## How every future *application / platform* is built

It is organised into the layer model (`00`, Part 5): governance, substrate, model, reasoning,
extension, experience — each crossing boundaries only through declared contracts (`03`).
Volatile pieces (frameworks, clouds, providers, models) are plugins at the edges (`11`); the
stable core is a small set of versioned contracts. State is partitionable; truth compounds
(`08`). Every mutation is a verified transaction (`02`).

## How every future *AI system* is built

It treats the model as truth and serves context per task — hard constraints always, freshness
always visible (`05`). It never trusts an agent about its own output (`10`). It records
provenance on every action, operates under least‑privilege grants (`13`), and keeps humans
accountable for significant and irreversible decisions (`00`, §3.6/§8.4).

## How every future *engineering team* works

By the ten engineering laws (`02`): understand before acting; audit before modifying; respect
the architecture; stay within scope; prefer reuse; prefer simplicity; separate
responsibilities; treat content as content; write for humans; verify before reporting success.
Every significant decision is an ADR. Every change passes the gate.

## How every future *designer* works

Within the design language (`01`): one institution, one language; tokens not literals;
accessible by construction; restraint over decoration. A design that fails accessibility is
not a candidate design.

## How every future *editor* works

By the editorial standards (`04`): clear, calm, precise, never overclaiming. Qur'an, Hadith
and Arabic are formatted correctly and sourced authentically; attributions are verified, not
assumed; clinical claims are sourced and disclaimered. Documentation is a projection of the
model, always current, never a parallel truth. Intellectual honesty over convenient sourcing,
always.

## How every future *product manager* works

By the decision framework (`00`, Part 7): classify decisions by reversibility and scope,
record them, and govern capability only once its governance exists (governance before
capability). Scope discipline is a first‑class tool. Humans remain accountable; AI assists.

## How every future *AI coding assistant* works

Cursor, Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, OpenAI Codex and their successors are **governed
participants, not privileged tools** (`10`). Each reads this operating system first and treats
it as authoritative. Each follows the same loop:

> audit → understand → implement within scope → verify (build, typecheck, lint, test, visual,
> accessibility, performance, security, docs) → produce a verification report → commit → tag →
> deploy behind the human gate.

None skips verification. None invents requirements or citations. None claims authority it was
not granted. None approves an irreversible production action. Each escalates rather than
guesses when requirements, documentation or architecture conflict.

## How this constitution changes

By addition and by tombstone, never by silent deletion. Ordinary clauses evolve through
recorded proposals. The non‑negotiables change only through the heightened amendment procedure
— super‑majority, cooling‑off, and a migration plan for everything affected. Evolution never
orphans the past; it re‑parents it. See `00`, Part 12 and `CHANGELOG.md`.

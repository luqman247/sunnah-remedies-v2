> In the name of Allah, the Most Merciful, the Most Compassionate.

This manual is the operative specification for photography and art direction at Sunnah Remedies. It extends **Part VIII (§33–§35)** of `Sunnah-Remedies-Design-Manual.md` and must be read alongside it. Where this manual is more specific, its specificity is binding. No photograph enters the institution's name without passing the Standard (§4 of the Design Manual) and the grammar defined here.

**This manual does not contain images.** It describes how images shall be made, graded, composed, and governed — so that any photographer, art director, or retoucher may execute without guessing.

---

## How to use this manual

- **Read §1–§3 before commissioning anything.** They define what every photograph must communicate and what it must never resemble.
- **Treat every numbered clause (§) as an instruction.** "Must" and "never" are binding.
- **Use the subject chapters (§10–§20)** when briefing a specific shoot — herbs, glassware, pilgrimage, and so on.
- **Every shoot requires a written brief** using the template in §22. No brief, no shoot.
- **When in doubt, refuse.** The institution prefers no image to a wrong image.

# §1 — Founding stance

§1.1 — Photography at Sunnah Remedies is **attestation, not advertisement**. An image names what is present. It does not promise what medicine cannot promise, does not manufacture desire, and does not perform wellness for the camera.

§1.2 — The created world is treated as *āyāt* — signs worth quiet attention. Matter (seed, oil, stone, page, glass) receives the dignity of a relic. The human, where present, receives modesty — never spectacle.

§1.3 — Every photograph must be able to sit **unchanged for twenty years** without dating the institution. If it would look wrong in a British Museum catalogue, a Wellcome archive, or an Aesop store in MMXXVI, it does not enter.

§1.4 — Photography is the **only sanctioned visual difference between departments** (Design Manual §7.3). Palette, type, and voice remain one institution. Differentiation is carried in subject-world and tonal temperature — never in a second brand look.

# §2 — Reference institutions — what each protects

Every photograph is judged against six references. A single clear failure disqualifies.

| Reference | What it protects | The photograph should feel like |
|---|---|---|
| **Aesop** | Material honesty; restraint; no manufactured desire | A vessel on stone; amber light; the product as object, not lifestyle |
| **Wellcome Collection** | Scholarship visible; specimen and archive; provenance before spectacle | A labelled jar; a hand-written index card; the herbarium drawer |
| **Monocle** | Editorial calm; global institution without tourism brochure | A corridor; a desk; a city seen from a considered distance |
| **Kinfolk** | Stillness; domestic craft without Pinterest softness | A table set for work, not for performance; linen, not staging |
| **Harvard Executive Education** | Authority without arrogance; the serious room | A seminar table; a lectern; scholars at work, not at pose |
| **British Museum** | Heritage; permanence; the object outlasting the photographer | A vitrine; raking light on patina; centuries visible in one frame |

§2.1 — **Synthesis.** The institution does not copy any one reference. It holds their shared discipline: **quiet, permanent, honest, unhurried.** If an image would suit a travel agency, a wellness influencer, a startup DTC brand, or a hospital marketing brochure, it fails — regardless of production quality.

# §3 — The six communicative qualities

Every published photograph must communicate all six. They are not optional moods; they are the institution's photographic character.

| Quality | Meaning in the frame | How it is achieved |
|---|---|---|
| **Calm** | No urgency; no visual shouting | Negative space; single subject; no motion blur; no crowded props |
| **Craftsmanship** | Human care visible in matter | Texture, tool marks, hand-finished edges, honest wear |
| **Scholarship** | Knowledge precedes spectacle | Books, labels, indices, graded specimens, citation visible |
| **Trust** | Nothing hidden; nothing exaggerated | True scale; true colour; limits visible; no retouch beyond §23 |
| **Heritage** | Continuity with tradition | Archival materials, classical architecture, patina, script |
| **Timelessness** | No trend, no era stamp | Natural light; muted palette; no filter fashion; no platform aesthetic |

§3.1 — **The twenty-year test.** Before publication, ask: *Would this photograph embarrass us in twenty years?* If yes — refuse.

§3.2 — **The Oxford test.** Before publication, ask: *Would this sit credibly beside a critical edition, a clinic intake room, and a museum label?* If no — refuse.

# §4 — The institutional grammar

These rules apply to **every** subject-world and every department.

§4.1 **Light.** Natural light only, with the exceptions in §5. No hard studio strobe aesthetic. No coloured gels. No ring-light catchlights.

§4.2 **Stillness.** Subjects are composed, not caught mid-gesture. The patience of the still life. No "decisive moment" photojournalism unless documenting an irreproducible rite — and even then, composed calm.

§4.3 **Margin.** The subject receives **deep frame margin** — never cropped to fill. Space around the object is reverence (Design Manual §33.1). On mobile, images are not re-cropped to bleed edge-to-edge.

§4.4 **Single subject.** One clear subject per frame unless the subject *is* a relationship (hands + book; herb + label) stated in the brief.

§4.5 **True colour.** Muted, accurate, institutional tonal temperature. No HDR, no saturation boost, no Instagram filters, no teal-and-orange grading.

§4.6 **Modesty.** Where the human appears: covered appropriately; never as body spectacle; never as diversity tokenism; never as stock "happy patient."

§4.7 **No implied claim.** An image must not imply a benefit the copy may not claim (Design Manual §33.4, §10.3). Honey is photographed as nourishment and means — never as cure. Hijāma as clinical discipline — never as miracle.

# §5 — Lighting

## 5.1 Doctrine

Light is the institution's primary retouching tool. **Get it right in camera.** Post-production adjusts; it does not invent atmosphere.

## 5.2 Preferred sources

| Source | Use | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **North light** | Still life, books, herbs, glassware | Soft, even, archival — Wellcome specimen |
| **Dawn / dusk** | Landscape, pilgrimage, architecture | Warm edge, long shadow — Kinfolk stillness |
| **Window light, single direction** | Clinical rooms, laboratories, desks | One soft directional fall — Aesop product |
| **Overcast daylight** | Outdoor materia medica, groves, stone | Low contrast, true colour — British Museum exterior |
| **Practical lamps** | Evening study, manuscript reading | Warm, local pool — Harvard seminar after hours |

## 5.3 Direction & quality

- **One dominant direction.** Side-light or three-quarter front preferred. Backlight only when the subject is translucent (honey, oil in glass) and flare is controlled.
- **Soft fall-off.** Transition from highlight to shadow is gradual — never cliff-edged flash.
- **Shadows are permitted and desired.** They give volume and honesty. Shadows are not "corrected away."
- **Specular highlights** on glass and metal: small, controlled, not blown — one or two points, not a disco.

## 5.4 Time of day by subject

| Subject | Preferred time | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Herbs & still life | Morning north window | Midday hard sun |
| Glassware & oil | Late morning side light | Flat overhead |
| Books & manuscripts | Overcast or north light | Direct sun on page |
| Clinical & laboratory | Morning window + ambient fill | Green-cast fluorescent alone |
| Architecture | Dawn, dusk, overcast | Harsh noon unless desert pilgrimage |
| Pilgrimage landscape | Dawn, blue hour | Golden-hour saturation cliché |

## 5.5 Artificial light — permitted only when

§5.5.1 — Documentary necessity (interior clinic, laboratory without sufficient daylight) **and** the brief states it.

§5.5.2 — Large soft source mimicking window: diffused, neutral (5500K–5600K), single direction. Must not read as "studio."

§5.5.3 — **Never:** ring light, beauty dish, coloured RGB panels, on-camera flash, multi-colour rim lights.

## 5.6 Exposure philosophy

Expose for **highlight retention** on skin, paper, and glass. Lift shadows in post only within §23 — never to a flat, lifeless grey.

# §6 — Colour grading

## 6.1 Alignment with the institutional palette

Photographs are graded toward the institution's earth-manuscript-metal world. They need not match hex values literally — but they must **harmonise** with Bone, Myrtle, Ink, Taupe, and Gilt when placed on the site.

| Palette token | Hex | Grading target in photography |
|---|---|---|
| Bone | #ECE6D6 | Paper whites, wall highlights — never pure #FFFFFF |
| Leaf | #E4DCC8 | Warm mid-tones in Apothecary still life |
| Ink | #23201A | Deep shadow, text, wood — not crushed black |
| Taupe | #6E6656 | Secondary surfaces, stone, aged linen |
| Ash | #9A9081 | Distant architecture, meta backgrounds |
| Myrtle | #1E2A22 | Deep green-black in foliage, clinic shadows |
| Gilt | #96763F | Warm accents: honey, brass, dawn edge — rationed |
| Rule | #D6CCB5 | Hairline separation in still life backgrounds |

## 6.2 Grading rules

§6.2.1 — **White balance:** Neutral to slightly warm. Never cold clinical blue unless the subject is a laboratory document (and even then, corrected toward honesty).

§6.2.2 — **Saturation:** Reduce 10–25% from camera default. Skin, herb, and stone stay true — not candy.

§6.2.3 — **Contrast:** Moderate. Micro-contrast on texture (linen weave, paper grain, seed surface) — not global punch.

§6.2.4 — **Blacks:** Lift slightly — Ink, not void. Detail retained in shadow.

§6.2.5 — **Highlights:** Roll off gently. No clipped specular except intentional (small glass point).

§6.2.6 — **Split toning:** If used, whisper only — warm highlights (#ECE6D6 family), cool shadows (Myrtle family). Must not read as "graded."

## 6.3 Department tonal temperature (within one grade)

| Department | Temperature shift | Reference feel |
|---|---|---|
| **Apothecary** | +warm: amber, honey, olivewood | Aesop + Wellcome specimen |
| **Academy** | Neutral-cool: ink, paper, shelf | Wellcome archive + Harvard room |
| **Sacred Journeys** | Atmospheric: stone, sky, dust | British Museum + Kinfolk landscape |

§6.3.1 — Temperature shifts are achieved in **lighting and subject**, not by separate LUT libraries per department.

## 6.4 Forbidden grading

- Teal & orange / blockbuster
- Cross-process / vintage filter fashion
- Heavy vignette
- Skin smoothing / beauty retouch
- HDR merge aesthetic
- Crushed blacks / milky fade (Instagram 2016)
- Neon saturation on herbs or food

# §7 — Lens choices

## 7.1 Doctrine

Lenses serve **truth of scale and truth of space**. The institution does not use extreme focal lengths for drama.

## 7.2 Primary kit

| Focal length (full-frame equivalent) | Role | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **35mm** | Environmental portrait, clinic room, architecture interior, pilgrimage context | Honest context; Monocle editorial |
| **50mm** | Still life, books, hands, glassware, herbs on table | Natural perspective; standard reference |
| **90–105mm macro** | Seed surface, honeycomb, script detail, label text | Wellcome specimen detail |
| **70–200mm** (used sparingly) | Architecture compression, distant minaret, ridge line | Sacred Journeys only; never for "hero zoom" |

## 7.3 Restricted use

| Focal length | Restriction |
|---|---|
| **24mm and wider** | Architecture and landscape only — never for product (distortion) |
| **135mm+** | Pilgrimage landscape only — never portraits (compression flattery) |
| **Macro below 1:1** | Detail inserts only — must include scale cue |

## 7.4 Aperture

- Still life & product: **f/4–f/8** — subject sharp, background soft but readable.
- Environmental: **f/5.6–f/8** — context legible.
- Portrait (when permitted): **f/4–f/5.6** — both eyes sharp; background recedes without bokeh balls as decoration.
- **Never:** f/1.4 product on table with creamy bokeh orbs as the subject.

## 7.5 Format

- **4:5 vertical** — Apothecary, Academy object (Design Manual §33.2)
- **16:9 horizontal** — Sacred Journeys landscape
- **1:1** — permitted for detail inserts only, not hero

# §8 — Composition

## 8.1 Principles

§8.1.1 — **Single subject, off-centre.** Rule of thirds or golden ratio — never dead-centre mugshot unless symmetrical architecture demands it.

§8.1.2 — **Generous negative space.** Minimum 30% of frame is quiet — wall, cloth, sky, out-of-focus shelf. The margin is part of the composition.

§8.1.3 — **Horizon level.** Architecture and landscape: level unless brief states otherwise. No dutch angle.

§8.1.4 — **Verticals true.** Building edges vertical; no wide-angle lean (correct in post if needed).

§8.1.5 — **Depth in layers.** Foreground quiet / subject / background soft — three planes maximum.

§8.1.6 — **No fill-the-frame cropping** on mobile hero. The institution crops in layout; the asset retains margin.

## 8.2 Compositional references

| Reference | Composition cue |
|---|---|
| Aesop | Object low in frame; stone or linen above |
| Wellcome | Specimen centred in vitrine logic; label visible |
| British Museum | Symmetry with human scale at edge |
| Kinfolk | Table seen from seated eye height |
| Monocle | Corridor leading line; person small in frame |
| Harvard Exec Ed | Table geometry; chairs implied; room authority |

## 8.3 Forbidden composition

- Hand holding product toward camera
- Flat lay knolling as Instagram pattern
- "Hero splash" liquid pour mid-air
- Overhead food-grid symmetry
- Person centred smiling at camera
- Before/after split frame
- Text overlay in image file (text lives in layout)

# §9 — Props

## 9.1 Doctrine

Props are **evidence of practice**, not decoration. Every prop must answer: *What work is being done here?*

## 9.2 Permitted prop categories

| Category | Examples | Departments |
|---|---|---|
| **Writing** | Dip pen, iron gall ink, blotter, index cards, folio | Academy |
| **Measurement** | Brass scales, glass measure, thermometer (period-appropriate) | Apothecary, Laboratory |
| **Containment** | Amber glass, ceramic dish, linen wrap, paper packet | Apothecary |
| **Text** | Open book, bookmark, marginalia (licensed/reproduction) | Academy |
| **Clinical** | Stethoscope (plain), cotton, gauze, steel tray | Clinical |
| **Travel** | Leather satchel (worn), map (folded), compass (not gimmick) | Sacred Journeys |
| **Natural** | Raw stone, branch, dried leaf (identified species) | All |

## 9.3 Prop rules

§9.3.1 — **Maximum three prop types** per frame besides the subject.

§9.3.2 — **No branded goods** except institution vessels where intentional.

§9.3.3 — **Patina over polish.** Objects show honest use — not prop-rental shine.

§9.3.4 — **No religious performance props** — no staged prayer rug arrangement for aesthetic alone; if prayer context, it is documentary and briefed.

§9.3.5 — **Labels and cards** are preferred props — they communicate scholarship (Wellcome).

## 9.4 Forbidden props

- Plastic wellness items (yoga blocks, smoothie jars, diffusers)
- Crystals as decoration
- Fresh flowers unless species-identified and subject-relevant
- Seasonal holiday décor
- Flags or nationalist symbols
- Luxury watch / jewellery as status
- Smartphone visible (unless documentary brief)
- Branded water bottles, coffee cups

# §11 — Backgrounds

## 11.1 Hierarchy of backgrounds

1. **In situ** — the actual room, grove, corridor, lab bench (preferred)
2. **Architectural ground** — stone, plaster, wood table (Aesop)
3. **Archival neutral** — warm grey, bone cloth, not white void
4. **Atmospheric distance** — sky, out-of-focus shelf, corridor depth

## 11.2 Background rules

§11.2.1 — **Never pure white (#FFFFFF)** except deliberate British Museum vitrine documentation — and even then, grade to Bone.

§11.2.2 — **Never saturated colour backdrop** — no teal, no millennial pink.

§11.2.3 — **Background supports subject** — one stop darker or softer than subject, not competing.

§11.2.4 — **Geographic honesty** — pilgrimage backgrounds are the actual place; not stock desert, not CGI mosque.

## 11.3 By department

| Department | Canonical background |
|---|---|
| Apothecary | Stone or linen table; out-of-focus dispensary shelf |
| Academy | Manuscript room; library stack; seminar wall |
| Sacred Journeys | Sky, stone, road, minaret at distance |
| Clinical | Painted wall, wooden door, simple curtain |
| Laboratory | Bench surface, muted cabinet, frosted window |

# §12 — People

## 12.1 Doctrine

People appear **as practitioners, scholars, travellers, patients — never as models performing wellness.** The human serves the institution's work; the camera does not consume the human.

## 12.2 When people appear

| Context | Permitted | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Scholar at desk | Yes | Three-quarter, eyes on work, not lens |
| Practitioner with patient | Yes, with consent | Clinical dignity; Mayo, not stock |
| Traveller on pilgrimage | Yes, from behind or profile | Small in landscape; modest dress |
| Hands only | Preferred (§13) | Craft and care |
| Faculty portrait | Rare; brief required | Plain ground; no glamour retouch |
| Group | Rare; institutional only | Formal distance; not crowd selfie |

## 12.3 People — binding rules

§12.3.1 — **Modest dress** in all frames. Coverage consistent with institution adab.

§12.3.2 — **No direct eye contact with camera** except formal faculty portrait (briefed).

§12.3.3 — **No smile directive.** Expression is neutral, contemplative, or engaged in task.

§12.3.4 — **Consent and release** recorded for every identifiable person (§25).

§12.3.5 — **No child** unless brief explicitly requires and guardian consent obtained.

§12.3.6 — **Diversity is not staged.** The institution documents who is present; it does not cast for representation.

## 12.4 Forbidden people photography

- Stock "happy patient" thumbs-up
- Doctor with arms crossed superhero pose
- Influencer wellness performance
- Tourist posing at sacred site
- Before/after body transformation
- Hijab as fashion prop without subject dignity
- Crowd in ḥaram as spectacle

# §13 — Hands

## 13.1 Doctrine

**Hands are the institution's preferred human presence.** They communicate craftsmanship, care, and scholarship without face spectacle.

## 13.2 Canonical hand subjects

| Action | Communicates | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Turning a page | Scholarship | Wellcome |
| Holding a pen mid-note | Study | Harvard |
| Weighing seed on brass scale | Craft, trust | Aesop + Wellcome |
| Pouring oil (static, low) | Means, not miracle | Aesop |
| Cupping preparation (clinical) | Clinical responsibility | Mayo |
| Planting or harvesting herb | Stewardship | Kinfolk |
| Folding a map | Pilgrimage | Monocle |

## 13.3 Hand styling

- **Clean, natural nails** — no salon aesthetic, no chipped neglect
- **No gloves** except clinical/laboratory when protocol requires
- **Skin tone true** — no retouch smoothing (§23)
- **Single pair** unless teaching gesture (briefed)
- **Age honest** — elder hands welcome; youth hands welcome

## 13.4 Forbidden hand shots

- Hands holding product toward camera (Design Manual §33.4)
- Manicured hands as beauty subject
- Multiple hands reaching for "community" stock trope
- Hand with pill bottle triumph gesture
- Surgical glove snap "action"

# §14 — Books

## 14.1 Doctrine

Books are **primary subjects** at the Academy — evidence of transmission (*isnād* made visible).

## 14.2 Book photography

| Treatment | Use |
|---|---|
| Closed spine, shelved | Faculty library; heritage |
| Open at readable page | Citation visible; legible type |
| Marginalia detail | Macro; scholar's hand optional |
| Stack with folio | Institutional publishing |
| Manuscript reproduction | Licensed only; no fake aging |

## 14.3 Book rules

§14.3.1 — **Page legibility:** if text is visible, it must be real, licensed, and accurately translated if quoted in caption.

§14.3.2 — **No faux manuscript** — aged paper stain kits refused.

§14.3.3 — **Open book lighting** — even across gutter; no half-page in shadow.

§14.3.4 — **Arabic script** set with respect — right-to-left orientation correct; not cropped mid-glyph for aesthetic.

§14.3.5 — **Bindings:** leather, cloth, thread — show craft. No cheap perfect-binding as hero.

## 14.4 Reference

Wellcome Collection archive photography + Harvard rare book room + British Museum reading room.

# §15 — Clinical environments

## 15.1 Doctrine

Clinical spaces are photographed as **Mayo Clinic meets Oxford college surgery** — competent, calm, dignified. Never as hospital marketing fear/hero narrative.

## 15.2 What to show

- Consultation room: two chairs, desk, plain wall, natural light
- Treatment room: couch, clean linen, instruments laid in order — not in hand
- Waiting area: wooden bench, bone wall, no TV screen
- Sterile tray: steel, gauze, cupping equipment — labelled, ordered
- Hand-washing basin: ceramic, not plastic commercial

## 15.3 Clinical rules

§15.3.1 — **No patient identifiable** without explicit clinical consent and brief.

§15.3.2 — **No procedure mid-blood** — clinical seriousness without spectacle.

§15.3.3 — **No "miracle cure" visual language** — no golden light on patient face post-treatment.

§15.3.4 — **Equipment honest** — real institution instruments; not props from unrelated trade.

§15.3.5 — **Signage** if visible: calm, institutional voice — not NHS panic poster collage.

## 15.4 Lighting & colour

Neutral window light; white balance corrected to true wall colour (warm white, not cyan). Grade toward Bone and Taupe — not cold hospital blue.

# §16 — Laboratories

## 16.1 Doctrine

The laboratory is **Wellcome specimen room meets Oxford chemistry** — scholarship through measurement, not sci-fi hub.

## 16.2 What to show

- Bench with glassware ordered by size
- Labelled sample jars (lot number visible where policy permits)
- Microscope at rest — not eyepiece POV gimmick
- Balance scale with brass weights
- Notebook open beside instrument — data handwritten
- Drying herb rack — identified species tags

## 16.2 Laboratory rules

§16.2.1 — **Clean but not sterile glamour** — working lab, not Apple Store.

§16.2.2 — **Labels legible** — lot, date, species where not confidential.

§16.2.3 — **No coloured smoke, no neon liquid** — no chemistry theatre.

§16.2.4 — **PPE** only when actively documented; otherwise bench at rest.

§16.2.5 — **Window light** preferred over overhead fluorescent alone.

## 16.3 Forbidden

- Stock "scientist pointing at flask"
- Glowing green liquid
- Futuristic LED bench
- Clutter of unrelated branded equipment

## 17.3 Herb rules

## 17.4 Reference

Wellcome herbarium + Aesop product still life + British Museum botanical plate (for companion illustration, not photo mix).

## 18.4 Forbidden

- Hand squeezing dropper at camera

# §19 — Architecture

## 19.1 Doctrine

Architecture is photographed as **permanence and institution** — British Museum + Monocle + Harvard campus. The building outlasts the photographer.

## 19.2 Subjects

- Institution facade — symmetrical or three-quarter; no fisheye
- Colonnade, corridor, arch — leading line; person optional small scale
- Courtyard — dawn; stone colour true
- Library interior — shelves, ladder, reading table
- Mihrāb, minaret, dome — from distance; no tourist postcard saturation
- Dispensary interior — shelf, counter, bone wall

## 19.3 Architecture rules

§19.3.1 — **Verticals corrected.** §8.1.4.

§19.3.2 — **No HDR sky** — clouds natural; not nuclear sunset.

§19.3.3 — **Human scale** — door, step, or distant figure for proportion.

§19.3.4 — **Sacred architecture** — photographed with adab; not as exotic tourism (§20).

§19.3.5 — **Renovation honest** — show patina and repair; no faux heritage.

# §20 — Pilgrimage

## 20.1 Doctrine

Pilgrimage photography is **Sacred Journeys subject-world** — landscape and place, not travel agency brochure. Kinfolk stillness + British Museum gravitas + Monocle distance.

## 20.2 What to show

- Road, path, wadi — empty or traveller from behind
- Stone, minaret, dome — atmospheric distance
- Lodge room — simple bed, book on table
- Grove path — dawn mist low, not saturated
- Camp at dusk — low tents, small fire (if safe and permitted)
- Footwear at door — departure implied, not staged

## 20.3 Pilgrimage rules

§20.3.1 — **No luxury hotel photography** — no pool, no buffet, no haram-view marketing.

§20.3.2 — **No crowd spectacle in sacred space** — if ḥaram appears, distance, respectful, not drone unless permitted by law and brief.

§20.3.3 — **Travellers modest; faces not hero** — profile, back, wide shot.

§20.3.4 — **No "spiritual glow" post** — no added light rays in retouch.

§20.3.5 — **Honest difficulty** — heat, dust, simplicity visible; not romanticised suffering.

§20.3.6 — **Gallery on site uses gilt line studies** until commissioned photography replaces — line studies are manuscript companion, not travel stock (current product state).

## 20.4 Forbidden pilgrimage imagery

- Airline seat and passport flat lay
- Suitcase influencer airport
- Resort swimming pool
- Group selfie at kaʿbah
- Camel ride tourism hero
- Stock desert with model in designer abaya

# §22 — Brief template & commissioning

No shoot proceeds without a completed brief approved against §4 (Design Manual).

## 22.1 Mandatory fields

```
INSTITUTION PHOTOGRAPHY BRIEF — SR-PHOTO-[YEAR]-[SEQ]

1. Subject (what is in frame)
2. Department & subject-world (§21)
3. Communicative qualities emphasised (§3 — pick primary two)
4. Ratio & minimum resolution (§7.5, Design Manual §35.2)
5. Light plan (time, direction, source — §5)
6. Lens & aperture (§7)
7. Composition note (subject position, margin % — §8)
8. Props (item list — §9)
9. Background (§11)
10. People / hands (yes/no; consent plan — §12–§13)
11. Mood (one word: calm / archival / atmospheric / clinical)
12. Reference frame (which of §2 applies most)
13. What this image must NOT imply (binding — §4.7)
14. Alt text draft (Design Manual §35.3)
15. Delivery specs (§23)
16. Approver signature
```

## 22.2 Commissioning hierarchy

1. **Institution-owned commission** — preferred; full rights; archive forever
2. **Photographer assignment** — work-for-hire or licensed exclusive
3. **Stock** — refused unless no alternative and brief documents exception; never generic wellness/travel

# §23 — Post-production & delivery

## 23.1 Permitted retouch

- White balance correction
- Exposure adjustment within true light
- Minor dust spot removal on sensor
- Vertical straightening (architecture)
- Shadow lift ≤15% without grey flatness
- Sharpening for output size — not crunchy

## 23.2 Forbidden retouch

- Skin smoothing / liquify
- Object removal changing meaning
- Sky replacement
- Added light rays, glow, bokeh
- Saturation beyond §6
- Composite without brief stating composite
- AI generation / inpainting of subject matter

## 23.3 Delivery

| Deliverable | Specification |
|---|---|
| Master | RAW or highest bit depth; archived permanently |
| Print | TIFF or PNG, Adobe RGB or ProPhoto, full margin |
| Web | sRGB, 2× largest placement width, margin preserved |
| Naming | `sr-[dept]-[subject]-[version]-[ratio].[ext]` |
| Sidecar | Brief ID, date, photographer, release status, alt text |

# §24 — Refused list

The institution **never publishes** photographs that exhibit:

1. Lifestyle wellness performance
2. Stock smiles and direct-to-camera happiness
3. Hands holding product toward lens
4. Travel-brochure saturation and postcard skies
5. Sterile white e-commerce void (except documented archival)
6. Before/after medical transformation
7. Fear-based clinical imagery
8. Influencer flat lay / knolling trend
9. HDR hyper-real landscape

11. CGI or AI-generated subject matter passed as photography
12. Sacred site tourism spectacle
13. Luxury hospitality as pilgrimage promise
14. Neon, gel, coloured flash aesthetics
15. Any image implying cure, miracle, or benefit beyond evidence

# §25 — Governance

§25.1 — This manual is amended only through the process in Design Manual §41. Silent drift is forbidden.

§25.2 — **Asset library.** No image enters the product outside the managed library (Design Manual §35.4).

§25.3 — **Releases.** Model, property, and location releases stored with asset ID.

§25.4 — **Review gate.** Every asset passes: Standard (§4) + grammar (§4) + refused list (§24) + alt text (§35.3).

§25.5 — **Photographer onboarding.** Every commissioned photographer receives §1–§4, §24, and the relevant subject chapter before shoot day.

§25.6 — **Relationship to illustration.** Where photography is not yet commissioned, **botanical line studies** in gilt stroke serve (Design Manual §34) — never stock photography as temporary compromise without documented exception.

§25.7 — **Page-level acquisition.** Every route's required assets and photographer briefs are specified in `Image-Acquisition-Plan.md`. Asset IDs in that plan are binding filenames and library keys.
